Problem: Three Deficiencies of Implicit Latent World Models

Identified Pain Points

  1. Inadequate compression: LAW and World4Drive retain large, uncompressed visual token sets, limiting tractable long-horizon world modeling.
  2. Weak spatial understanding: Latent WMs lack 3D geometry; World4Drive requires an external depth estimator at inference time, adding latency.
  3. Underutilized temporal dynamics: Prior methods predict only $T+1$ from frame $T$; historical ego status is not conditioned into the world model.

Latent-WAM's Response

  1. Learnable queries compress multi-view images into 16 scene tokens per view via DINO cross-attention — compact by design.
  2. WorldMirror geometric features distilled into DINO backbone at training time; no extra module at inference.
  3. Causal Transformer conditioned on full historical Scene-Ego status; multi-step prediction with stride-4 horizon.

Architecture: Three-Module Pipeline

1

Spatial-Aware Compressive World Encoder (SCWE)

Multi-view images $I \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times M \times H \times W \times 3}$ (T=4, M=3, 448×224 px) fed into DINOv2-Base. Learnable scene queries $Q_{\text{scene}} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times M \times 16 \times 768}$ concatenated with image patch tokens; encoder projects to $D_l=256$, yielding 16 compact scene tokens per view per frame.

Simultaneously, frozen WorldMirror extracts patch-level geometric features $f_g(I) \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times M \times S \times 2048}$ (pre-computed offline). Cosine similarity loss aligns DINO patch tokens with WorldMirror features via geometric projector $\phi$:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{align}} = 1 - \cos(\text{LN}(\phi(\hat{X})),\; \text{LN}(f_g(I)))$$

2

Dynamic Latent World Model (DLWM)

Scene tokens + ego status (command, velocity, acceleration encoded by single-layer MLP) form Scene-Ego world status $S_{\text{world}} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times (M \cdot 16 + 1) \times 256}$. A 4-layer causal Transformer with 3D-RoPE (temporal freq 50, camera freq 10, token freq 100) predicts future world status $S_{\text{future}}$, supervised by an EMA-updated frozen copy of SCWE. Frame-wise teacher-forcing: intra-frame bidirectional attention; cross-frame strictly causal.

Ego status predicted from $S_{\text{future}}$ via three MLPs (command: cross-entropy; velocity/acceleration: MSE). Only used during training.

3

Trajectory Decoder

Learnable trajectory queries $Q_\tau \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times n_p \times 256}$ attend to current world status $S_{\text{world}}^t$ via 4-layer Transformer decoder. MLP decodes to $K$ candidate trajectories $(x, y, \theta)^{n_p}$ over 4-second horizon. Driving command $C$ selects the final trajectory $\tau$. Only SCWE + Trajectory Decoder run at inference time.

Total loss: $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{traj}} + 0.1\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{align}} + 0.2\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{wm}} + 0.1\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{ego}}$

Critical Analysis

Highlights

  • Geometric distillation pre-computes WorldMirror features offline — zero inference-time overhead for spatial awareness.
  • ~10x training data reduction vs. Drive-JEPA while matching or exceeding EPDMS (Figure 1).
  • LoRA degradation result (DINO-Base-LoRA: 68.5 EPDMS) is a useful negative finding: high-dimensional geometric distillation requires full fine-tuning flexibility.
  • Zero-shot HUGSIM transfer demonstrates cross-distribution generalization without any domain adaptation.

Weaknesses

  • DLWM and its outputs are entirely discarded at inference; its influence on planning is indirect (backbone co-training only). This design gap is unstated.
  • Consistent "Latent-WMA" vs. "Latent-WAM" naming error throughout experiment tables and figures — unacknowledged in the text.
  • HUGSIM comparison is asymmetric: Latent-WAM is zero-shot; all baselines trained with HUGSIM data.
  • No variance across runs; fine-grained EPDMS differences (89.0 vs. 89.1 vs. 89.3) have no statistical grounding.
