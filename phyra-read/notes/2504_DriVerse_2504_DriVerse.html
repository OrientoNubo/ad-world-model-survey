2. Research Overview

What the paper does

DriVerse generates future driving videos conditioned on a single input image and a 3D trajectory. Trajectory information is encoded in two complementary forms: discrete trend tokens inserted into text prompts (semantic channel) and projected 3D static anchor motions in 2D image space (spatial channel). A Latent Motion Alignment module adds inter-frame consistency supervision for dynamic objects. A Dynamic Window Generation strategy handles sharp heading-angle changes at inference time.

Domain Tags

Driving World Models / Video Generation / Trajectory-conditioned Control / Diffusion Transformer

Key Architectures
  • DiT backbone (Wan2.1-based): frozen base; trainable copy receives control
  • MTP: trend tokenization + TSA spatial anchors
  • LMA: CoTracker v3 + motion-weighted latent loss
  • DWG: anchor-visibility-driven frame selection at inference

4. Problem and Motivation

Storyline

Driving world models must simulate future scenes under given trajectories for planning and closed-loop training. Layout-conditioned models require expensive annotations (p.2). Text/discrete-signal models lack trajectory precision (p.2). Trajectory-conditioned models that directly encode 3D coordinates or project them to 2D suffer from misalignment with pretrained 2D generative features, degrading temporal consistency during turns and lane changes (p.2-3). DriVerse decomposes trajectory conditioning into a semantic channel (trend tokens in text) and a spatial channel (anchor-point motion in 2D space), with separate modules for dynamic object consistency and sharp-turn handling.

Module 1 — Trend Tokenization (MTP)

Maps each trajectory segment direction to one of 12 clock-direction tokens by quantizing the azimuthal angle into 30° sectors. Tokens are embedded into text prompts using a fixed inline template — no new special tokens required.

$$\text{token}_t = \langle T_{\text{quantize}(\theta_t)} \rangle \quad \theta_t = \angle(\Delta \mathbf{x}_t)$$
Module 2 — Trajectory-Guided Spatial Anchors (TSA)

Static 3D anchor points are uniformly distributed around the ego vehicle at frame 0, then projected into each frame's image plane using ego-poses and camera intrinsics. Trailing projections with transparency decay encode motion history. Motion vectors are color-mapped as optical-flow-style control signals injected into the trainable DiT copy.

$$\mathbf{u}_j^t = \pi(\mathbf{K} \cdot \mathbf{T}_t \cdot \mathbf{X}_j^0) \qquad \alpha_j^{t-m} = \exp(-\lambda \cdot \|\mathbf{u}_j^t - \mathbf{u}_j^{t-m}\|_2)$$
Module 3 — Latent Motion Alignment (LMA)

CoTracker v3 tracks pixel-wise correspondences in dynamic regions. A motion-weighted latent consistency loss is computed in VAE feature space rather than pixel space, giving higher weight to points with larger displacements.

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{cons}} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i \sum_{t=1}^{T} \|z_t(p_i^t) - z_0(p_i^0)\|_2^2 \qquad w_i = \frac{\sum_t \|p_i^t - p_i^0\|_2}{\sum_j \sum_t \|p_j^t - p_j^0\|_2}$$
Module 4 — Dynamic Window Generation (DWG)

At inference time, anchor visibility ratio $V_t = |A_t|/|A_0|$ is computed each frame. If at least 60% of anchors remain visible throughout the window, generation continues from the last frame. Otherwise, the earliest frame where visibility drops below 60% becomes the new conditioning frame, preventing stale pixel conditioning in sharp turns.

$$I_{\text{key}} = \begin{cases} I_N & \text{if } V_t \geq 0.6 \; \forall t \\ I_{t^*} & \text{otherwise, } t^* = \arg\min_t \{V_t < 0.6\} \end{cases}$$

Fundamental Limitations
  1. 60% anchor visibility threshold for DWG is a heuristic; no ablation on this value
  2. LMA tracking depends on generation quality early in training (chicken-and-egg)
  3. Trend vocabulary loses speed information (fast and slow straight motion get same token)
  4. Single front-camera only; multi-view consistency not addressed
  5. No closed-loop integration demonstrated; utility for policy learning is asserted but not shown
Citations Worth Tracking
  1. Vista [14] (NeurIPS 24): primary baseline; architecture and training scale determine the significance of DriVerse's improvements
  2. CoTracker v3 [36] (arXiv 24): LMA quality is bounded by tracking accuracy in urban scenes
  3. GenAD [67] (CVPR 24): Vista's base; explains the strong starting point
  4. DrivingDiffusion [41] (ECCV 24): by first author; DriVerse's autoregressive inference follows its approach
  5. Wan2.1 [62] (arXiv 25): DiT backbone; its pretraining scope bounds what DriVerse can learn

8. Open Questions and Improvement Ideas

Outstanding Questions
  • Actual training data volume vs Vista — does the gap hold at equal data?
  • Is the 60% anchor visibility threshold principled or empirically tuned?
  • Can LMA use ground-truth frames for tracking to decouple from generation quality?
  • How does quality degrade with trajectory type (straight vs. turn vs. U-turn) at 30s?
Improvement Directions
  1. Speed-aware tokens: add slow/medium/fast quantization alongside direction tokens; resolves speed-information loss with no architecture change
  2. Multi-view extension: apply TSA projection across multiple cameras for cross-view geometric consistency
  3. Closed-loop integration: use GAE as trajectory-fidelity reward proxy in end-to-end training
  4. GT-based LMA tracking: run CoTracker on real video frames to decouple tracking quality from generation quality
