Motivation

Problem and Core Argument

Driving world models offer scene representations via future prediction, motivating their use for planning. Two existing strategies — explicit future generation (high latency) and latent world models (low interpretability) — both share a root cause: vision and motion representations are optimized independently. Scene simulators optimize perceptual reconstruction; planners regress actions in isolation. This representational schism prevents planners from inheriting motion priors embedded in world model training.

Core argument: The latent features sufficient to generate the future should be identical to those used to decide the future. Once a trajectory vocabulary enforces structure during WM training, both visual and motion encoders become directly inheritable by a downstream planner.

Method Detail

Core Module Formulas

Multi-modal Trajectory Encoder

Trajectory vocabulary V: 256 K-means anchors from nuPlan + nuScenes. Top-K nearest anchors retrieved; residuals computed.

$$c = E_a(V_K) + E_o(Y - V_K)$$

TA-DWM Training Loss

Standard latent diffusion denoising objective.

$$\mathcal{L}_{world} = \mathbb{E}_{z_0,t,\epsilon}\!\left[\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(z_t;f,t,c)\|_2^2\right]$$

Planning Transformer Decoder

$$Q_p = D_{plan}(Q_a, [f, e], [f, e])$$

FAR Distillation + Rewarder

$$\hat{z}^k = D_{scene}(Q_s,[f,c^k],[f,c^k])$$ $$\mathcal{L}_{align} = \mathbb{E}_k\!\left[\|\hat{z}^k - \text{SG}(z^k)\|_2^2\right]$$ $$\mathcal{L}_{reward} = -\mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\sigma(r_{pos}-r_{neg})\right]$$

Fundamental Limitations

  • Vocabulary coverage ceiling: 256 K-means anchors from nuPlan/nuScenes; rare maneuvers outside clustering range cannot be represented structurally by the anchor+offset scheme.
  • Single-view depth ambiguity: Monocular front-facing input; multi-view occupancy methods retain a structural sensing advantage that cannot be recovered without architectural changes.
  • FAR domain sensitivity: Future-scene decoder supervised only on nuPlan/nuScenes distribution; distilled future features may not correspond to physically plausible futures in out-of-distribution deployment domains.
Open Questions

Outstanding Issues and Improvement Directions

Unresolved Questions

  • Why does K=10 degrade safety metrics? Only described qualitatively; causal mechanism not verified.
  • Sensitivity of PDMS to vocabulary size N (fixed at 256, inherited from WoTE).
  • Cross-dataset transfer of FAR distillation without retraining.

Improvement Directions

  1. Vocabulary-free motion encoding (high feasibility): continuous encoder end-to-end trained removes coverage ceiling.
  2. Multi-view extension (moderate): TA-DiT accepts spatial inputs; BEV projection adapter would resolve depth ambiguity.
  3. Online FAR adaptation (lower): lightweight few-shot fine-tuning with self-supervised signal for domain shift.
