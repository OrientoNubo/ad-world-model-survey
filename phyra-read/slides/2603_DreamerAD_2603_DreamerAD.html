Method Overview

DreamerAD has two coupled components: World Model + Latent Reward Modeling and RL with Vocabulary Sampling.

[8x Cameras] ──► [DCAE Encoder] ──► Latents Z
[Hist. Traj]  ──► [MLP Encoder]  ──► Action emb a

Z + a ──► [Temporal Projection] ──► E
Last frame E ──► [SF-WM (1-step)] ──► z_hat_next (latent rollout)

[Traj DiT] ──► tau_act (base policy)
tau_act + sigma² ──► [Gaussian Vocab Sampling] ──► {tau_sample} (G candidates)

{tau_sample} ──► [SF-WM frozen rollout x8] ──► {z_hat_1..8}

r_pred ──► temporal weighting ──► r_final
r_final ──► [GRPO] ──► L_actor + L_bc + L_kl ──► policy update

Core Modules

1. Shortcut Forcing World Model (SF-WM)

Goal: Compress Epona's 100-step flow matching to 1–4 steps via teacher-student distillation without degrading latent quality.

Mechanism: Step size $d$ is sampled from $1/\mathcal{U}(\{1,2,4,\ldots,K_{\max}\})$. For $d > d_{\min}$, two teacher half-steps compute $x_{\text{mid}}$ and velocities $v_1, v_2$; the stop-gradient average $\text{sg}((v_1+v_2)/2)$ becomes the training target. Loss is weighted by $\omega(t) = 0.9t + 0.1$.

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x_0,x_1,t,d}\left[\omega(t)\|\phi_\theta(x_t,t,d)-v_{\text{target}}\|^2\right]$$

Training: 12 epochs, 3 days, 32 H20 GPUs. Inference: VisDiT steps=1, TrajDiT steps=20.

2. Autoregressive Dense Reward Model (AD-RM)

Goal: Predict 8 reward dimensions at 8 temporal horizons from latent world model states, enabling dense temporal credit assignment.

Architecture: Eight independent learnable bases $Q_{\text{base}} \in \mathbb{R}^{8 \times D}$ modulated by trajectory and step embeddings. Cross-attention over compressed history (latent dim $L=512$ compressed to $l=32$), followed by MLP head.

$$r_{\text{pred}}^t = \text{MLP}(\text{Cross-Attention}(Q_r, \text{his}_{-3:t}))$$

Vocabulary filtering: From 8192 candidates, retain trajectories satisfying $|\Delta y| \leq 5\text{m}$, $|\Delta x| \leq 10\text{m}$, $|\Delta\theta| \leq 20°$. Final vocabulary size $K=256$.

3. Gaussian Vocabulary Sampling

Rank vocabulary candidates by Mahalanobis distance to base policy $\tau_{\text{act}}$:

$$d(x,\tau_{\text{act}})=\sum_{t,i}\frac{(x_{t,i}-\tau_{\text{act}_{t,i}})^2}{\sigma_{t,i}^2}$$

Mixed strategy: $g_1$ by softmax (discrimination) + $g_2$ from Gaussian neighborhood (local exploration). Avoids ODE/SDE mismatch of Flow-GRPO and dynamic discontinuity of WorldRFT.

4. GRPO Policy Optimization

Safety reward $L$ uses log-sigmoid aggregation over {NC, DAC, DDC, TLC}. Task reward $S$ uses $\log(\sum w_j r_j)$. Dense aggregation:

$$r_{\text{final}}=\sum_{t=1}^{8}w_t \cdot r_{\text{total}}^t$$

Final objective: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{actor}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{bc}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{kl}}$

Training: 2 epochs, ~8 hours, lr=1e-4, batch=196.

Fundamental Limitations

  • Vocabulary filtered relative to ground-truth trajectories; structurally cannot explore globally novel maneuvers without rebuilding the vocabulary.
  • SF-WM distillation must be repeated for each new backbone or dataset; not transferable out of the box.
  • Reward quality bounded by SF-WM accuracy for OOD actions; not tested.
  • Full pipeline requires ~2+ weeks of H20 GPU compute across four sequential training stages.

Open Questions

  • Does the 80x latency claim hold for the full pipeline (including Traj DiT at 20 steps), or only the VisDiT component?
  • How does reward model quality degrade as the SF-WM rollout horizon extends beyond the 4-second training range?
  • Would a larger or dynamically expanded vocabulary further improve EPDMS, or does sampling saturate early?
  • Can the latent RL framework transfer to CARLA or nuPlan test-challenge scenarios without full reward model retraining?
