Problem and Motivation

Current driving world models are trained predominantly on safe expert demonstrations, causing them to hallucinate when simulating non-expert or hazardous actions. This prevents their use for policy evaluation tasks that require simulating the consequences of suboptimal decisions.

Pain PointImpact
Expert-only training data (safety/regulatory constraints)Cannot simulate crashes, off-road, or hazardous outcomes
Poor action controllability (e.g., Vista fails on non-expert steering)Cannot perform what-if analysis for different action choices
No reward signal from simulated futuresGap between visual simulation and decision-making remains open

ReSim's core argument: the bottleneck is a data imbalance between expert and non-expert scenarios, which can be closed by integrating CARLA simulator data with real-world driving logs (p. 1-2).

Key Contributions

1 Heterogeneous Data Compilation: First to systematically integrate CARLA non-expert data with real-world expert data (OpenDV + NAVSIM) for driving world model training, improving action controllability across both expert and non-expert actions (p. 1, 3).
2 Controllable World Model: CogVideoX-based diffusion transformer with dynamics consistency loss, unbalanced noise sampling, and progressive multi-stage learning. Achieves high-fidelity simulation under diverse action conditions (p. 3-4).
3 Video2Reward Module: DINOv2-based reward estimator trained on CARLA infraction scores, bridging simulated video and scalar reward for planning and policy selection applications (p. 4-5).

Key Technical Innovations

  • Dynamics Consistency Loss: Penalizes discrepancies in latent motion across K=4 time intervals, enforcing temporal coherence
  • Unbalanced Noise Sampling: Increases sampling frequency in [500, 1000] from 1/2 to 2/3, preventing shortcut learning at low-noise timesteps
$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{dynamics}} = \mathbb{E}_{x,\epsilon,t}\left[\sum_{j=1}^{K}\sum_{i}^{N-j}\frac{1}{s}\left\|(d^{i+j} - d^j) - (x^{i+j} - x^i)\right\|^2\right]$$

Video2Reward (V2R) Module

A standalone reward model that converts ReSim's simulated video into a scalar reward, enabling reward-based driving applications without manual reward engineering.

ComponentDetail
BackboneFrozen DINOv2 (per-frame visual features)
Head2 spatial-temporal attention blocks + MLP for scalar output
SupervisionCARLA Infraction Score (penalizes collisions, traffic violations, off-road, low speed)
Training20 epochs, 35K CARLA samples, AdamW, LR 1e-6, input 224 x 224

At inference: a candidate trajectory is rendered by ReSim into video, then V2R scores it. The trajectory with the highest reward is selected for execution.

Fundamental Limitations

  • V2R trained solely on CARLA infraction scores; real-world reward transfer is not demonstrated beyond NAVSIM
  • Inference takes 2 min per 4s video on A100 (p. 20), preventing real-time closed-loop use
  • Closed-loop simulation (Fig. 8) is demonstrated qualitatively only, without quantitative metrics

Key Takeaways

  • Heterogeneous data (real expert + simulated non-expert) is a practical strategy for improving world model reliability across the full action space
  • The Video2Reward pathway creates a usable bridge between visual simulation and reward-based decision-making
  • Zero-shot generalization results (nuScenes FID 5.2, FVD 50.4) demonstrate that the model's visual quality transfers across datasets
  • The primary bottleneck for practical deployment is inference speed (2 min / 4s video), which the authors acknowledge as future work
