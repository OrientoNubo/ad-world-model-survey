Core Problem: Supervision Deficit

VLA models map high-dimensional visual inputs to a few low-dimensional action signals (waypoints). This creates a supervision deficit: the vast model capacity is supervised by sparse, low-dimensional actions, leaving representational power underutilized.

Consequence 1

Large VLA models can underperform smaller specialized BEV models (Table 1, p.7)

Consequence 2

Simply scaling action-only data does not improve performance; baselines plateau at 70M frames (Table 3, p.9)

Solution: World Modeling as Dense Self-Supervision

DriveVLA-W0 supplements action prediction with future image prediction as a dense, self-supervised objective. This forces the model to learn transferable visual representations of the driving environment.

  • VQ variant (Emu3 backbone): Autoregressive next-token prediction of discrete visual tokens
  • ViT variant (Qwen2.5-VL backbone): Latent diffusion model conditioned on VLA features to generate future images

The world model is active during training only. At inference, it is bypassed for action-only output.

Architecture Overview

AR World Model (VQ)

Predicts discrete visual tokens of the current frame autoregressively, conditioned on past observations and actions.

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{WM-AR}} = -\sum_{i=1}^{N} \log P(v_i | S_{<V_t}, v_{<i})$$

Trained jointly: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{Total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{Action}} + \alpha \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{WM-AR}}$

Diffusion World Model (ViT)

Denoises future image latents conditioned on VLA visual and action features.

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{WM-Diff}} = \mathbb{E}_{z, \epsilon, k} \left[ \| \epsilon - \hat{\epsilon}(z_{t+1,k}, k, F_t^V, F_t^A) \|^2 \right]$$

Trained jointly: $\mathcal{L}_{\text{Total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{Action}} + \beta \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{WM-Diff}}$

The MoE Action Expert uses Joint Attention to fuse its features with the VLA backbone, reducing inference latency from 117.8ms to 74.3ms (63.1% reduction, p.10).

Performance reversal at scale: Query-based wins on small data (NAVSIM 103K); autoregressive wins on large data (in-house 70M). The autoregressive decoder's teacher-forced training scales more effectively with data complexity (Table 4, p.9).

At 70M frames, world modeling improves ADE by 28.8% (VQ) and reduces collision rate by 19.9% (ViT). Baselines plateau while world-model-augmented models continue improving. (Table 3, p.9)

Key Ablation Findings

Vision-Action Grounding

6VA pretraining (85.6 PDMS) outperforms 6V vision-only (84.1) and no pretrain (80.7). Grounding visual predictions in ego actions is critical for learning causal dynamics. (Table 5, p.10)

Sequence Length

Longer context improves performance: VA 83.3, 2VA 84.2, 6VA 85.6 PDMS. Temporal context is necessary for learning long-horizon dynamics. (Table 6, p.10)

Generative Fidelity

Higher FID quality (4.61 for 6VA vs. 9.85 for 2VA) correlates with better planning (85.6 vs. 84.1 PDMS). World model quality directly impacts downstream performance. (Table 8, p.16)

Temporal Interval

1-second interval is optimal (85.6 PDMS). No history (82.9) lacks dynamics; 4-second gap (84.3) introduces excessive variation. (Table 9, p.17)

Limitations and Open Questions

  • World model discarded at inference: The trained world model is used only as a training regularizer; no test-time planning or simulation benefit is realized
  • Proprietary data for core claim: The 70M-frame scaling law is demonstrated on a non-public dataset, limiting reproducibility
  • Single camera only: Does not address multi-camera or multi-sensor fusion needed for production systems

Improvement Directions

  1. Use the world model at inference for look-ahead planning or trajectory re-ranking
  2. Validate scaling law on public large-scale datasets (e.g., full NuPlan)
  3. Compare world modeling against other dense auxiliary tasks (depth, segmentation, optical flow) to isolate the source of benefit
