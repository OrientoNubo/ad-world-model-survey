DriveDreamer: Towards Real-world-driven World Models for Autonomous Driving

Xiaofeng Wang, Zheng Zhu, Guan Huang, Xinze Chen, Jiagang Zhu, Jiwen Lu
GigaAI, Tsinghua University | arXiv: 2309.09777v2 | Nov 2023
World Model Autonomous Driving Video Generation Diffusion Model Action Prediction

Core Contributions

  1. First real-world-driven world model: DriveDreamer is the first world model built entirely from real-world driving data (nuScenes), in contrast to prior works relying on gaming or simulated environments (p.1).
  2. Autonomous-driving Diffusion Model (Auto-DM): A diffusion model conditioned on structured traffic information (HDMaps, 3D boxes, text prompts) that generates controllable driving videos with high fidelity (p.2).
  3. ActionFormer: A GRU-based module that iteratively predicts future traffic structural conditions from driving actions, enabling interaction with the environment and future state prediction (p.6).

Problem and Motivation

World models for autonomous driving promise to generate driving videos and driving policies for safe maneuvering. However, prior work (MILE, Iso-Dream, LSTM-based models) focused predominantly on gaming or simulated environments, lacking representation of real-world driving complexity (p.1-2).

Modeling the real world introduces a vast search space: dynamic foreground objects, static background, and intricate traffic structural information all need to be captured simultaneously. DriveDreamer addresses this by using structured traffic conditions as an intermediate representation to constrain the generation process.

Two-Stage Training Pipeline

Stage 1: Single-frame structured conditions -> Auto-DM - Learn traffic structural understanding - HDMaps + 3D Boxes + Text as conditions - No temporal attention (structural focus only) Stage 2: Multi-frame video prediction -> Auto-DM + ActionFormer - Add temporal attention layers - ActionFormer predicts future structural conditions - Enable environment interaction via driving actions

The two-stage design separates structural comprehension (Stage 1) from temporal dynamics (Stage 2), which the authors claim enhances sampling efficiency and accelerates convergence (p.3-4).

Auto-DM Architecture

Auto-DM takes three types of control conditions as inputs (p.4-5):

Spatially Aligned Conditions

  • HDMaps $H \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W \times 3}$: lane boundaries, dividers, pedestrian crossings
  • Encoded via convolution layers, concatenated with noisy latent $Z_t$

Position Conditions

  • 3D Boxes $B \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times N_b \times 16}$ with categories $C \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times N_b}$
  • Aggregated via Fourier embedding + CLIP + gated self-attention

Text prompts (weather, time of day) are incorporated through cross-attention layers. Temporal attention layers $\mathcal{F}_t$ maintain frame coherence across the generated video (p.5).

$$H^p = \mathcal{F}_a([C_e, \text{Fourier}(B)])$$ $$v = v + \tanh(\eta) \cdot \text{TS}(\mathcal{F}_s([\nu, H^p]))$$

ActionFormer

The ActionFormer addresses a key limitation: during video prediction beyond the present timestamp, future traffic structural conditions are unavailable (p.6).

Initial conditions (I_0, H_0, B_0) -> Encode + Flatten -> 1D latent -> Self-attention + MLP -> hidden state h_0 -> Cross-attention with action features F_ca -> GRU iterative prediction: h_{t+1} = F_GRU(h_t, s_t) -> Decode to future structural conditions

Key design: GRU-based recurrent prediction operates in feature space rather than pixel space, mitigating noise interference. The latent variable $s_t$ is sampled from a learned Gaussian distribution for variational inference (p.6, Eq. 5).

Ablation and Key Findings

  • Two-stage training matters: First-stage training alone improves FID from 15.9 to 15.3 and FVD from 363.3 to 349.6, demonstrating that structural comprehension before temporal dynamics learning is beneficial (Table 2, p.8).
  • ActionFormer over concatenation baseline: Using ActionFormer to iteratively predict future conditions outperforms directly concatenating action features with zero-padded conditions, yielding the best FID (14.9) and FVD (340.8) (Table 2, p.8).
  • Synthetic data augmentation: 4K synthetic images improve BEVFusion mAP by 3.0 points, a meaningful gain for 3D detection (Table 1, p.8).
  • Collision rate: DriveDreamer reduces average collision rate by 21% compared to VAD in open-loop planning (Table 3, p.9).

Controllable Generation Capabilities

Text-conditioned Style

Text prompts control weather (sunny, rainy) and time-of-day (daytime, night) of generated videos while preserving traffic structure (Fig. 1, 9, p.1, 14).

Action-conditioned Interaction

Different driving actions (turn left, turn right, go straight) produce distinct future video trajectories from the same initial frame (Fig. 1, 10, p.1, 15).

The model also extends to multi-view generation (Fig. 6-8) by replacing frame-wise attention with view-wise attention, achieving view-consistent and frame-consistent outputs (p.12).

Limitations and Open Questions

  • Single-dataset evaluation: All experiments use nuScenes only; generalization to other driving datasets or domains remains untested.
  • Closed-loop gap: Only open-loop planning is evaluated; the world model's value for closed-loop driving policy learning is not demonstrated.
  • Resolution constraint: Video generation at 128x256 is far below practical deployment requirements; the upscaling to 1600x900 for synthetic data relies on separate fine-tuning.
  • Temporal horizon: 16-frame prediction (~2.7s at 6Hz) is short for meaningful long-horizon planning.
  • Dependence on perception annotations: HDMaps and 3D boxes come from pre-existing perception systems (LAV, BEVerse, UniAD); the model does not learn these from raw sensor data.
