Problem & Motivation

Existing driving world models face two core challenges in long-term video generation:

ChallengeCauseConsequence
Accumulated ErrorRecurrent frameworks recursively condition on predicted frames (Fig. 2a, p.2)Quality degrades over time; Vista fails after ~8 seconds (Fig. 1a)
Scene Consistency LossInitial condition influence diminishes in later batches (p.2)Generated frames lose coherence with the original scene description
Anchor Frame ArtifactsDivide-and-Conquer frameworks produce imperfect anchor frames (p.5, Fig. 5)Artifacts propagate to interpolated high-FPS frames

Core Idea: Coarse-to-(Re)fine

MiLA rethinks the Divide-and-Conquer paradigm by adding an error correction mechanism for anchor frames:

Condition Frames + Waypoints + Camera + Text | v [Coarse Stage: MiLA DiT] --> Low-FPS Anchor Frames | v [(Re)fine Stage: MiLA DiT x2] |-- JDC: Add calibrated noise to anchors, jointly denoise |-- TPD: Progressive denoising (frames near conditions denoise faster) | v Corrected Anchors + High-FPS Frames --> Multi-view Long-term Video

The (Re)fine stage recurrently leverages both previous high-FPS frames and Coarse-stage anchor frames as conditions, maintaining temporal continuity across batches.

Joint Denoising and Correcting Flow (JDC)

Predicted anchor frames are treated as noisy approximations of ground truth. JDC adds calibrated noise so anchors can be jointly denoised alongside interpolation frames:

Step 1: Model predicted anchor as noisy version:

$$z = (1 - \alpha_1)x_0 + \alpha_1 n_1, \quad n_1 \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$$

Step 2: Add second noise layer with calibrated variance:

$$x_n = (1 - \alpha_2)z + \alpha_2 n_2, \quad n_2 \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$$

The variance $\sigma^2 = \frac{\alpha_2 - 2\alpha_1\alpha_2 + 2\alpha_1}{\alpha_2}$ ensures the combined noise follows a standard normal distribution (Eq. 11, p.5).

$\alpha_1$ controls correction strength: too large destroys scene structure, too small fails to correct dynamic object artifacts (Fig. 7, p.7).

Temporal Progressive Denoising (TPD)

Frames closer to conditions have higher initial fidelity and need fewer denoising steps. TPD assigns per-frame schedules via a cosine function:

$$f(t, \phi_s) = 1 - \frac{\cos((1-t)\omega + \phi_s) - \cos(\phi_s)}{\cos(\omega + \phi_s) - \cos(\phi_s)}, \quad \phi_s = 0.5\pi \cdot \frac{s}{|S|}$$

This produces a slow-fast pattern for trailing frames and fast convergence for leading frames, visualized in Fig. 4b (p.5).

Phyra Critical Assessment

Highlights

  • The Coarse-to-(Re)fine framework addresses a real limitation of both Recurrent and Divide-and-Conquer paradigms through recurrent refinement with error correction
  • JDC provides a principled theoretical derivation (Eq. 8-12) for anchor frame noise calibration
  • State-of-the-art on nuScenes across both short and long-term metrics

Weaknesses

  • Single dataset evaluation (nuScenes only); generalizability to other driving datasets is unverified
  • TPD scheduler is heuristic (cosine function); no analysis of optimality or comparison with alternative schedules
  • No inference speed or computational cost analysis despite 700M parameters on 64 A100s
  • "Without degradation" claim for long-term generation is overclaimed; Fig. 4a shows quality still varies with temporal distance

Key Equations

Rectified Flow (foundation, Eq. 4-7):

$$x_t = (1-t)x_0 + tx_1, \quad \frac{d}{dt}x_t = v(x_t, t), \quad \hat{x}_1 = x_0 + \int_0^t v(x_t, t)dt$$

Multi-view Spatial Attention (Eq. 3):

$$h_{\text{spatial}}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d}}\right) \cdot V, \quad Q, K, V \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times (VdHdW) \times C}$$

Loss Function (Eq. 15):

$$\sum \int_0^1 \mathbb{E}[(1-m_s) \odot \|y - v(x_{f(t,\phi_s)}, f(t,\phi_s))\|^2 + m_s \|y - v(x_{g(t)}, g(t))\|^2] dt$$

Fundamental Limitations

  • Batch boundary discontinuity: Even with JDC correction, each batch boundary is a potential source of temporal discontinuity that can only be mitigated, not eliminated
  • Noise distribution assumption: JDC assumes anchor frame errors follow the same distribution as diffusion noise (Eq. 8). Systematic errors (e.g., consistent color shift) may violate this assumption
  • Quadratic multi-view cost: Spatial attention over all views (Eq. 3) scales as $O(V^2 \cdot (dH \cdot dW)^2)$, limiting scalability to higher view counts
  • Waypoint dependency: No mechanism to handle waypoint uncertainty or noisy trajectory inputs
