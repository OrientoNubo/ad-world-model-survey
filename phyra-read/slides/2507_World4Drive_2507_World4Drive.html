Problem and Motivation

End-to-end autonomous driving typically relies on costly perception annotations (3D bounding boxes, HD maps). Recent latent world models (e.g., LAW) attempt self-supervised learning from raw images, but face two core limitations:

  • Single-modal latent features from raw images fail to capture spatial-semantic structure of the 3D physical world, leading to slow convergence (Figure 1, p.1)
  • Ignoring multi-modal driving intentions prevents the world model from evaluating alternative futures, limiting planning quality under complex scenarios

World4Drive addresses both issues by incorporating vision foundation model priors for physical world understanding and coupling driving intentions with latent world modeling.

Core Contributions

  1. Physical World Latent Encoder: Integrates spatial priors (Metric3D v2 depth) and semantic priors (Grounded-SAM) with temporal aggregation to produce physically grounded latent representations
  2. Intention-aware World Model: Generates multi-modal trajectories conditioned on different driving intentions, predicts future latent states per intention, and selects the best trajectory via self-supervised latent reconstruction
  3. Perception-free SOTA: Achieves state-of-the-art E2E planning without perception annotations on both nuScenes (open-loop) and NavSim (closed-loop) benchmarks

Architecture Overview

Multi-view Images I_t | v [Context Encoder] -- Grounded-SAM (semantic) + Metric3D v2 (depth) | v [Temporal Aggregation] -- Cross-attention with previous frame features | v L_t (Physical World Latent) | +--- Q_plan (from Intention Encoder: K-means on trajectory vocab) | v [World Model Dreamer] -- Cross-attention + MLP action encoder | v T = {T^1,...,T^K} multi-modal trajectories + L_{t+n} predicted future latents | v [World Model Selector] -- Min feature distance to actual future latent | v Final planning trajectory T^j

The two key modules, Driving World Encoding and Intention-aware World Model, are tightly coupled: the physical latent provides the world state, and the intention-aware dreamer imagines multiple futures for the selector to evaluate.

Physical World Latent Encoder

The encoder enriches raw image features with three layers of understanding:

  • Semantic Understanding: Grounded-SAM produces pseudo semantic labels; cross-entropy loss $\mathcal{L}_{sem}$ enhances semantic awareness (Eq. 2, p.4)
  • 3D Spatial Encoding: Metric3D v2 estimates multi-view depth; forward projection obtains 3D positions; sinusoidal PE + MLP produces spatial embeddings $E_t = \text{MLP}(\text{SPE}(P_t))$ (Eq. 3, p.4)
  • Temporal Aggregation: Cross-attention between current and previous frame features captures dynamic changes: $L_t = \text{CrossAttention}(\hat{F}_t, \hat{F}_{t-1})$ (Eq. 4, p.4)

Intention-aware World Model

Two sub-components work together to generate and select trajectories:

World Model Dreamer

  • K-means clusters trajectory endpoints into 3 commands (left, right, straight) x K intentions each (K=6 default)
  • Cross-attention aggregates scene context into multi-modal trajectories: $T = \text{MLP}(\text{CrossAttention}(Q_{plan}, L_t))$ (Eq. 5)
  • Action encoder + cross-attention predicts future latents per intention: $L_{t+n} = \text{CrossAttention}(Q_{future}, \text{Concat}(A, L))$ (Eq. 6)

World Model Selector

  • Compares predicted future latent with actual future latent (detached) for each modality
  • Training: selects modality with minimum feature distance; trains ScoreNet with focal loss
  • Inference: selects trajectory with highest ScoreNet score

Training Objective

The final end-to-end training loss combines four terms:

$$\mathcal{L} = \alpha \mathcal{L}_{sem} + \beta \mathcal{L}_{recon} + \gamma \mathcal{L}_{score} + \eta \mathcal{L}_{traj}$$

with $\alpha = 0.2$, $\beta = 0.2$, $\gamma = 0.5$, $\eta = 1.0$ (Eq. 8, p.5).

  • $\mathcal{L}_{sem}$: Semantic cross-entropy loss (Grounded-SAM pseudo labels)
  • $\mathcal{L}_{recon}$: Reconstruction loss between predicted and actual future latents
  • $\mathcal{L}_{score}$: Focal loss for ScoreNet training
  • $\mathcal{L}_{traj}$: L1 loss guiding the selected trajectory against expert trajectory

Limitations and Open Questions

  • VFM inference cost: Running Grounded-SAM and Metric3D v2 at inference likely incurs computational overhead comparable to explicit perception modules; no latency analysis is provided
  • Single-step prediction: World model predicts only at timestep t+n (default n=3), not multi-step rollouts; limits long-horizon temporal reasoning
  • Fixed intention discretization: K-means clustering on trajectory endpoints is a one-time preprocessing step; no adaptive refinement based on scene context
  • Selector gap: Training uses oracle latent matching; inference relies on learned ScoreNet proxy; the gap between the two is not analyzed
  • NavSim gap: DiffusionDrive achieves 88.1 PDMS vs. World4Drive's 85.1 (Table 2); this gap is not discussed in the paper

Improvement Directions

  1. Multi-step latent rollout: Extend to sequential t+1, t+2, ..., t+n predictions for richer temporal reasoning
  2. Adaptive intention generation: Replace fixed K-means with a learned intention proposal network conditioned on scene context
  3. VFM distillation: Distill Grounded-SAM and Metric3D v2 knowledge into the backbone during training, eliminating VFM dependency at inference
  4. Selector consistency loss: Minimize the gap between oracle latent matching and ScoreNet via an explicit consistency objective
