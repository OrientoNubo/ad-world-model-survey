Autonomous Driving Scene Generation Semantic Occupancy Multi-view Video LiDAR Diffusion

Core Idea

UniScene is the first unified framework that generates three key data forms for autonomous driving from BEV layouts: semantic occupancy, multi-view video, and LiDAR point clouds.

The key insight: decompose the joint distribution into a two-step occupancy-centric hierarchy:

$\mathbb{P}(\text{Vid}, \text{Lid} | \text{BEV}) = \mathbb{P}(\text{Vid}, \text{Lid} | \text{Occ}) \cdot \mathbb{P}(\text{Occ} | \text{BEV})$

Semantic occupancy serves as a rich intermediate representation that captures 3D geometry and semantics, bridging the gap between coarse BEV layouts and high-fidelity sensor data.

Architecture Overview

[BEV Layouts] | v [Occupancy VAE + DiT] --> [3D Semantic Occupancy] / \ v v [Gaussian Rendering] [Sparse UNet] [Semantic + Depth] [Voxel Features] | | v v [Video Diffusion] [Prior Guided Sampling] [+ Text Prompt] [+ Ray Rendering] | | v v [Multi-view Video] [LiDAR Points]

Stage 1: Generate temporally consistent 3D semantic occupancy from BEV layouts via Occupancy VAE and Diffusion Transformer.

Stage 2: Condition two parallel branches on the occupancy to produce multi-view video (via Gaussian-based rendering) and LiDAR point clouds (via prior-guided sparse modeling).

Stage 1: Occupancy Generation

Occupancy VAE

  • 2D CNN encoder with axial attention compresses 3D occupancy (H x W x D) into latent space
  • Learnable class embedding $C^s$ for each semantic category
  • Continuous compression (VAE) outperforms discrete (VQVAE): 92.1 vs. 75.2 mIoU at comparable ratios (Tab. 2, p.6)

Occupancy DiT

  • Stacked spatial and temporal transformer blocks for spatio-temporal aggregation
  • BEV layouts concatenated with noise volumes as conditioning
  • Temporal attention enables frame-consistent occupancy sequence generation

Stage 2a: Video Generation

Gaussian-based Joint Rendering

  • Occupancy grid converted to 3D Gaussian primitives, rendered into semantic maps and depth maps
  • Bridges the domain gap between 3D occupancy and 2D image conditioning
  • Rendered maps serve as rich conditioning for the video diffusion model

Geometric-aware Noise Prior

  • Depth maps warp appearance priors from reference frames to other views
  • Injects explicit geometric awareness into the diffusion sampling process
  • Reduces FVD from 87.52 to 70.52 (Tab. 9, p.8)

Stage 2b: LiDAR Generation

Occupancy-based Sparse Modeling

  • Sparse UNet: encodes occupancy into sparse voxel features, avoiding computation on empty space
  • Prior Guided Sampling: samples points along rays guided by occupancy priors (probability 1 for occupied voxels, 0 elsewhere)
  • Ray-dropping Head: simulates realistic LiDAR behavior where rays fail to return

Key Ablation Findings

Occupancy Model (Tab. 8, p.8)

  • DiT Spatial Attention is the most critical component: removing it drops mIoU from 19.44 to 10.29
  • Temporal attention in DiT reduces MMD by 38.04% (from 17.10 to 10.60)

Video Model (Tab. 9, p.8)

  • Occupancy-derived semantic maps and depth maps are more effective than spatial-temporal attention alone for cross-view consistency
  • Geometric-aware noise prior reduces FVD by 19.4% (from 87.52 to 70.52)

LiDAR Model (Tab. 10, p.8)

  • Prior guided sparse sampling reduces memory by 69.7% (16.66 to 5.05 GB) while improving JSD by 4.0%
  • Ray-dropping head improves JSD by 28.0% (0.100 to 0.072)

Limitations and Open Questions

Fundamental Limitations

  • Sequential pipeline with no feedback: occupancy errors propagate uncorrected to video and LiDAR
  • Occupancy resolution (200 x 200 x 16) limits fine geometric detail below voxel scale
  • No cross-modal consistency constraint between generated video and LiDAR
  • Video resolution limited to 256 x 512; generalization tested qualitatively only

Improvement Directions

  • Joint training or iterative refinement with feedback from downstream branches
  • Cross-modal consistency loss between video and LiDAR outputs
  • Higher-resolution or hierarchical occupancy representations
  • Few-step diffusion or distillation for faster occupancy generation
