Problem and Motivation

Existing driving world models face a fundamental tension between two paradigms:

  • Video diffusion models (e.g., Vista) model the joint spatiotemporal distribution of fixed-length clips, preventing flexible-length generation and trajectory integration (p2)
  • GPT-style autoregressive models discretize images into tokens, losing spatial correlations and high-frequency details through quantization (p2, Fig. 3)

Root cause: these approaches conflate temporal dynamics modeling with fine-grained visual/trajectory generation, two tasks with fundamentally different computational characteristics.

Core Idea: Decoupled Spatiotemporal Factorization

Epona reformulates world modeling as a sequential future prediction process in continuous latent space, factoring the problem into:

  1. A temporal context encoder (MST) that compresses historical observations into compact latents
  2. Two specialized diffusion generators that operate frame-by-frame conditioned on this context:
    • TrajDiT: generates future trajectory plans
    • VisDiT: generates the next video frame

This separation naturally supports autoregressive variable-length generation while preserving continuous spatial fidelity.

Architecture Overview

Historical frames {O_t} --[VAE]--> Latents {Z_t} | Trajectories {a_{t-1->t}} -----+ | v v [MST: 12-layer, 1.3B params] Interleaved spatial + causal temporal attention | F (compact latent) / \ v v [TrajDiT: 2-layer] [VisDiT: 12-layer] 50M params 1.2B params Trajectory plan Next frame latent {a_{T->T+N}} | v [Temporal-aware DCAE] | Next frame O_{T+1} | [Autoregressive feedback loop]

Total model size: 2.5B parameters (p6)

Module 1: Multimodal Spatiotemporal Transformer (MST)

Encodes historical visual latents $\mathbf{Z} \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times T \times L \times C}$ and action sequences $\mathbf{a} \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times T \times 3}$ into a compact context $\mathbf{F} \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times (L+3) \times D}$.

  • Interleaved causal temporal attention (across T frames) and multimodal spatial attention (across L+3 positions per frame)
  • Causal mask ensures each frame attends only to past; naturally supports variable-length context
  • Progressive information incorporation reduces memory compared to full-sequence attention

12 layers, 1.3B parameters

Module 2 & 3: Dual Diffusion Transformers

TrajDiT (Trajectory Planning)

  • Generates N-frame continuous trajectory plans via rectified flow diffusion
  • Dual-Single-Stream DiT architecture (Appendix A)
  • Loss: $\mathcal{L}_{traj} = \mathbb{E}\left[\|\mathbf{v}_{traj}(\bar{\pi}(t), t) - (\bar{\pi} - \epsilon)\|^2\right]$

VisDiT (Visual Prediction)

  • Generates next-frame latent conditioned on MST context F and action control $\mathbf{a}_{T \to T+1}$
  • Action injected via MLP modulation (scale and shift)
  • Total loss: $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{traj} + \mathcal{L}_{vis}$

TrajDiT: 2 layers / 50M params; VisDiT: 12 layers / 1.2B params

Chain-of-Forward Training

Addresses autoregressive drift caused by the gap between teacher-forcing training and autoregressive inference.

  • Every 10 training steps, perform 3 forward passes using model-predicted frames as input
  • One-step estimation shortcut: $\hat{x}_{(0)} = x_{(t)} + t \cdot v_\theta(x_{(t)}, t)$ (Eq. 7, p6)
  • Simulates inference-time noise accumulation, helping the model adapt to its own prediction errors

Validation (Fig. 8, p8): FID gap between w/ and w/o chain-of-forward grows beyond 15 autoregressive frames, confirming the strategy is increasingly beneficial for long-term generation.

Ablation Studies

Shared Latent for Joint Prediction (Table 5, p8)

Removing video prediction drops NAVSIM PDMS from 86.2 to 78.1, confirming that jointly modeling video and trajectory in a shared latent space benefits planning.

Chain-of-Forward Training (Fig. 8, p8)

FID gap grows beyond 15 autoregressive frames; validated on NuPlan test set only.

Evaluation Summary

Strengths

  • Principled decoupled formulation that enables both flexible-length generation and real-time planning from a unified architecture
  • Chain-of-forward training with validated effectiveness for long-term autoregressive quality
  • Emergent traffic rule understanding from self-supervised prediction (Fig. 1C, p1)

Weaknesses

  • Front-camera only; competing planners use multi-view input with auxiliary supervision
  • No closed-loop evaluation; real driving requires error compounding assessment
  • Ablations spread across different benchmarks; no unified ablation table

Overall Score

4 / 5

Key Takeaways

  1. Decoupling temporal dynamics from fine-grained generation is an effective strategy for building flexible, long-horizon driving world models
  2. Diffusion-based continuous trajectory generation is a viable alternative to discrete token-based planning, achieving competitive or superior results
  3. Chain-of-forward training is a practical and validated approach to mitigate autoregressive drift in world models
