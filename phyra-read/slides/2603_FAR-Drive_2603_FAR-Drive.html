FAR-Drive

Frame-AutoRegressive Video Generation in Closed-Loop Autonomous Driving

arXiv 2603.14938 2026-03-16 Zhejiang University & Huawei Closed-Loop Simulation Autoregressive Video Diffusion Transformer

Research Overview

FAR-Drive trains a frame-level autoregressive multi-view video generator as a closed-loop simulator for autonomous driving. Given per-frame structured controls (BEV map, 3D bboxes, ego-motion, camera params, text) and a variable-length history, the model generates the next-frame multi-view observation at 576×1024 resolution across 6 cameras. A two-stage training strategy addresses long-horizon consistency (ARHC) and exposure bias (blend-forcing). System-level inference optimizations bring per-frame latency below one second on a single GPU.

Core Architecture
  • MMDiT backbone (~2.5B): SD3-based; causal attention for AR
  • Cross-view attention (~0.3B): Interleaved every 4 blocks
  • Control DiT (~0.8B): ControlNet-style; zero-init injection
  • DC-AE VAE (1×32×32): Spatial-only; per-frame encoding
Three Core Challenges
  • Long-horizon temporal & cross-view consistency
  • Autoregressive degradation (exposure bias)
  • Sub-second inference latency
Total Parameters

~3.7B (backbone 2.5B + cross-view 0.3B + control 0.8B + misc 0.1B)

Problem & Motivation

Core gap: Existing video generators are open-loop and cannot respond to per-step agent actions. AR generators suffer from exposure bias — pure teacher-forcing creates a train-test distribution mismatch; pure self-forcing amplifies drift. FAR-Drive addresses both within a unified training recipe.

Physical Insight (from paper, Sec. 1)

A single reference frame provides only positional information. Two frames enable velocity estimation. Three or more frames enable acceleration inference. Adaptive reference-horizon conditioning exploits this progression.

Methodology

Data Flow

[History frames XHt] → [DC-AE VAE encode] → [Latent KV Cache]
[Future frame noise zt]                             |
[Controls Ct: BEV, bbox, camera, motion, text]      |
    → [Convolutional encoder]                       |
    → [Condition Cache]                             |
    → [Control DiT (3 units)]                       |
         ↓ zero-init residual inject                |
[Backbone MMDiT (28 blocks, causal + cross-view)] ←─┘
    → [Denoised latent X̂t+1]
    → [DC-AE VAE decode]
    → [Next-frame 6-view RGB 576×1024]
    → [Feed back as history for t+2]

Stage I: Adaptive Reference-Horizon Conditioning (ARHC)

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ARHC}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{l,t}\left[\left\|\mathbf{u}_\theta(\mathbf{z}_t, t \mid \mathbf{X}_{1:l}) - \mathbf{u}^*(\mathbf{z}_t, t \mid \mathbf{X}_{1:l})\right\|_2^2\right]$$

$l$ sampled from $\{0,\ldots,L-1\}$ with bias toward short horizons ($l=1,2,3$). $l=0$: text-to-video; $l=1$: image-to-video; $l>1$: video-to-video. Sequence length $L=16$.

Stage II: Blend-Forcing Autoregressive Training

$$\tilde{\mathbf{X}}_i = \alpha \hat{\mathbf{X}}_i + (1-\alpha)\mathbf{X}_i$$
$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{BF}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{t}\left[\left\|\mathbf{u}_\theta(\mathbf{z}_t, t \mid \tilde{\mathbf{X}}_{1:t}) - \mathbf{u}^*(\mathbf{z}_t, t \mid \tilde{\mathbf{X}}_{1:t})\right\|_2^2\right]$$
  • $\alpha$ starts at 0 (full GT); grows linearly at $1\times10^{-4}$/step; reaches 1.0 after 10,000 steps
  • Training converges at ~7,000 steps; one standard FM step per blend-forcing step
  • AR rollout randomly extended up to 16 frames; only last 8 frames used for loss

Control DiT Injection

$$c^{(0)} = x^{(0)} + \mathrm{Proj}_0(u), \quad c^{(l+1)} = \mathcal{C}^{(l)}(c^{(l)}), \quad x^{(l+1)} = \mathcal{B}^{(l)}(x^{(l)}) + \mathrm{Proj}_l(c^{(l)})$$

3 control units (vs. 7 backbone units); injection into early backbone layers only; zero-init Proj preserves pretrained backbone at start of training.

Inference Acceleration

Techniques
  • Sampling step reduction: Blend-forcing enables 3-step inference without quality collapse (prior: noisy output at low steps)
  • Frame-level VAE (1×32×32): Spatial-only; no temporal entanglement; per-frame independent encoding
  • Latent KV cache: Reference frame keys/values cached; only new frame contributes fresh queries
  • Condition cache: Control features encoded once per target frame; reused across all denoising steps

Fundamental Limitations

Fixed linear alpha schedule in blend-forcing has no mechanism to respond to degradation onset during training; schedule sensitivity is untested.
Evaluation is nuScenes-only; structured control signals (BEV/bbox formats) are dataset-specific; generalization requires re-engineering the control pipeline.
Real-time operation (~10 fps) requires ~0.1 s/it — 8-10x below current reported latency; real-time closed-loop use is not achievable under this architecture.
No policy agent interacts with the simulator; "closed-loop" is demonstrated at the video generation level only, not as a full training loop.

Open Questions & Improvement Directions

Open Questions
  • What is ARHC's independent contribution isolated from blend-forcing?
  • Would an adaptive alpha schedule (conditioned on rollout FVD) outperform the fixed linear schedule?
  • Can the framework be extended to LiDAR or occupancy outputs for policy evaluation?
  • What is the downstream policy performance (L2, collision) when an agent trains inside FAR-Drive?
Improvement Directions (by feasibility)
  • (High) Standalone ARHC ablation: run without stage I to quantify its contribution
  • (Medium) Adaptive alpha schedule: monitor rollout FVD during training; slow growth on degradation
  • (Medium) Downstream policy eval: wrap as gym env; measure L2/collision for IL/RL agent
  • (Low) Consistency distillation: single-step AR model toward real-time generation
