Self-Driving with Learned Object-Centric Representations

Shadi Hamdan, Fatma Guney · Koc University, KUIS AI Center · arXiv 2407.15843 · 2024-07

Autonomous Driving Object-Centric Slot Attention Transformer CARLA

Core Idea

Replace hand-crafted object attribute vectors with self-supervised slot representations extracted via SAVi from BEV sequences, then use a modified autoregressive transformer to jointly learn driving actions and predict future scene dynamics.

Motivation: Why Slots?

Representation Problem
Scene-level BEV (VQ-VAE) High-dimensional (192x192x8), most pixels are road/background; discretization loses object-level detail, resulting in DS 17.07 (Table 1)
Exact Object Attributes (PlanT) Requires access to ground-truth position, size, heading, speed for each object; incomplete for non-vehicle objects
Learned Slots (this work) Self-supervised, implicitly capture spatiotemporal context; no explicit attribute engineering needed

Architecture Overview

Two-Stage Pipeline

  • Stage 1 (SAVi): Frozen slot attention model extracts K=30 slots (d=128) from T=2 BEV frames. Trained separately with a self-supervised reconstruction objective.
  • Stage 2 (CarFormer): Modified GPT-2 (H=768, 6 layers, 12 heads) processes a mixed sequence of discrete tokens (target point, traffic light, speed) and continuous tokens (slots, route vectors).

Block Attention

Replaces causal mask with a block triangular mask: slots within the same timestep attend bidirectionally, while causal ordering is maintained across timesteps. This enables inter-object reasoning within each time step.

Training Formulation

Imitation learning with two action heads and an auxiliary forecasting loss:

$$\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{wp} + 40 \cdot \mathcal{L}_{forecast}$$
  • GRU Head: Predicts W continuous waypoints; supervised with L1 loss
  • LM Head: Predicts quantized waypoints (k_q=48 bins) via cross-entropy
  • Forecasting: Predicts future slot features z_{t+f}; supervised with MSE loss (weight alpha=40)

Training: AdamW, lr=5e-5, batch=512, 100 epochs, 4x A6000/A100 GPUs (Supplementary Table 7)

Limitations and Open Directions

Structural Limitations

  • Relies on ground-truth BEV maps; not applicable to real sensor input without a BEV estimation pipeline
  • SAVi is frozen and trained separately; slot extraction errors propagate to driving with no corrective gradient
  • Slot-to-object binding is not guaranteed; hallucinated vehicles (false positives) are observed in forecasting results (p.13)

Improvement Directions

  • End-to-end joint training of SAVi + CarFormer
  • Replace GT BEV with learned BEV estimation (e.g., BEVFormer from camera input)
  • Use forecasting capability for model-based RL or planning via multi-step rollouts
