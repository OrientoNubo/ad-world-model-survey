Seeing the Future, Perceiving the Future

A Unified Driving World Model for Future Generation and Perception

Problem and Motivation

Existing Driving World Models (DWMs) focus on either RGB future generation or depth estimation, but not both. This leads to two gaps:

Generation-only DWMs

Models like Vista, DriveGAN, and DriveDreamer predict future RGB frames but ignore geometric structure. This causes structural incoherence at longer prediction horizons (p.1-2).

Perception-only Models

Depth estimators like Marigold and DepthAnything operate on single current frames. They cannot predict future scene geometry, limiting their use for planning (p.1).

Core argument: Image and depth are heterogeneous representations of the same 3D scene. A shared latent space with bidirectional interaction enables mutual reinforcement between generation and perception.

Method: UniFuture Overview

UniFuture builds on Stable Video Diffusion (SVD) and introduces two key mechanisms:

Dual-Latent Sharing (DLS)

Both RGB images and depth maps are encoded into a shared latent space using a single pre-trained VAE encoder/decoder. No separate depth autoencoder is needed. This enforces cross-modal consistency by design (p.4).

Multi-scale Latent Interaction (MLI)

Bidirectional feature interaction between image and depth representations at 4 UNet scales (full, 1/2, 1/4, 1/8). Uses depth layers, inside feedback (zero-conv), and outside feedback (p.4-5).

Architecture: Training and Inference Pipeline

Training Pipeline

Image seq x ──► Shared Encoder E ──► z_(x) ──► Denoising Video UNet ──► denoised latent ──► Shared Decoder D ──► Output images │ ▲ MLI ◄────┘ │ │ Bidirectional Depth seq d ──► Shared Encoder E ──► z_(d) ──► MLI ──────────────► predicted z^_(d) ──► Shared Decoder D ──► Output depth

Inference Pipeline

Single current image is encoded, concatenated with (M-1) Gaussian noise frames, and denoised through the Video UNet with MLI to produce M-1 future image-depth pairs simultaneously (p.5).

Multi-scale Latent Interaction (MLI)

MLI contains three components at each UNet scale:

  • Depth layers: Concatenate UNet encoder/decoder features, reduce dimensionality, combine with previous scale output, upsample
  • Inside feedback: Zero-initialized convolution feeds depth information back into the UNet stages
  • Outside feedback: Post-UNet convolution aligns final depth prediction with input latent

Key Strengths

  • First unified model achieving competitive results on both future generation and depth perception simultaneously
  • Shared VAE approach is simple and effective, reducing model complexity
  • Strong zero-shot transfer to Waymo without fine-tuning

Key Weaknesses

  • Depth pseudo-labels from DepthAnythingV2 create a ceiling; evaluation against the same teacher raises circularity concerns
  • No inference latency analysis; computational overhead of MLI is unknown
  • No explicit 3D reasoning despite claims of geometric awareness; operates entirely in 2D latent space

Limitations and Open Questions

Fundamental Limitations

  • Depth accuracy bounded by DepthAnythingV2 pseudo-label quality
  • Single-camera, forward-facing only; no surround-view support
  • No semantic understanding (labels, tracks, motion fields)
  • Implicit 2D geometry, not explicit 3D scene representation

Open Questions

  • How does depth quality degrade beyond 12-frame horizon?
  • Would LiDAR ground truth training improve results?
  • What is the inference latency overhead of MLI?
  • How does the model handle occlusion/dis-occlusion?
