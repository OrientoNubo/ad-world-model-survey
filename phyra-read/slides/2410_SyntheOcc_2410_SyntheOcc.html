Problem and Motivation

Core problem: Existing controllable image generation methods for autonomous driving condition on BEV layouts or 3D bounding boxes, which lack fine-grained 3D geometric control at the voxel level.

Root cause: A representation gap between 3D occupancy (volumetric, irregular) and 2D diffusion models. Converting occupancy to 1D embeddings or 2D maps loses spatial structure and occlusion information.

Entry point: 3D semantic Multi-Plane Images (MPIs) as a bridge, encoding full 3D geometry into camera-aligned depth planes that are natively compatible with 2D diffusion decoders.

Method Overview

[3D Occupancy] --Eq.1-3--> [3D Semantic MPI (N x D x H x W)] | [MPI Encoder: Conv1x1 + ReLU + Conv1x1] | [Multi-scale spatial features] | v [Text Prompt] --> [Stable Diffusion UNet] <-- [Features added to Decoder] | [Cross-view Attention] (neighboring L/R views) [Cross-frame Attention] (temporal consistency) | [VAE Decoder] --> [Multi-view Street Images]

Three key components: (1) 3D semantic MPI construction from occupancy, (2) lightweight 1x1 conv MPI encoder for spatial feature injection, (3) reweighting strategies for long-tailed training.

Core Innovation: 3D Semantic MPI

Occupancy voxels are back-projected into camera frustum space and sliced into $D = 256$ depth planes spanning $d_{min} = 0$ to $d_{max} = 50$ meters. Each plane stores the semantic index of the nearest occupied voxel.

Key equations (p.4):

$$P_l = (u \times d_l,\; v \times d_l,\; d_l)^T, \quad d_l = d_{min} + (d_{max} - d_{min}) \times l / D$$ $$\text{MPI}_{n,l} = \text{Interpolate}(\text{Occupancy},\; T_n \cdot K_n^{-1} \cdot P_l)$$

Why MPI works: Each plane is a 2D image at a specific depth, directly compatible with 2D diffusion features. Collectively, the planes encode full 3D structure including occlusion, without the ambiguity of 2D projections.

MPI Encoder Design

A lightweight encoder using 1x1 convolutions (not 3x3) to extract features from the semantic MPI and inject them into the UNet decoder at multiple scales.

Why 1x1 over 3x3: Operations occur in camera frustum space, not Euclidean space. A 1x1 receptive field provides correct pixel-wise spatial alignment without mixing information across depths that are spatially distant in 3D (p.5).

Importance Reweighting

Three strategies to address foreground/background imbalance and long-tailed categories:

  • Progressive Foreground Enhancement: Cosine annealing from weight 1 to $m=2$ over training, gradually increasing foreground importance. $$w(x,m,n) = \frac{m-1}{2} \cdot (1 + \cos(\frac{x}{n}\pi + \pi)) + 1$$
  • Depth-aware Reweighting: Higher weights for farther foreground objects (adapting GeoDiffusion [3] with cosine function for stability).
  • CBGS Sampling [45]: Class-Balanced Grouping and Sampling to equalize category frequencies.

Cumulative ablation: 23.05 -> 24.40 -> 24.40 -> 25.50 mIOU (Tab. 3, p.8)

Claim Verification

  • "Finer 3D geometric control": Supported by qualitative (Fig. 1, 3, 6) and quantitative (Tab. 1, 2) evidence.
  • "Substantial image quality advancement": Partially supported; best FID (14.75), but Gen-setting mIOU (25.5) far below oracle (39.3).
  • "Effective data augmentation": Weakly supported; +1.0 mIOU improvement, non-scalable at 2x ratio.

Limitations and Open Questions

Structural Limitations

  • Layout dependency: Requires pre-existing occupancy annotations as input; cannot generate novel layouts from scratch.
  • Depth resolution ceiling: 256 planes over 50m gives ~0.2m resolution; finer geometry requires more memory.
  • Augmentation scalability: Declining benefit at 2x ratio (Tab. 6) indicates distribution gap that more data cannot close.

Open Questions

  • Why does augmentation benefit decline at 2x? No analysis of distribution shift vs. redundancy.
  • How robust is the method to noisy occupancy predictions (vs. ground truth labels)?
  • Can the MPI representation extend to non-uniform depth plane allocation for better near-range detail?
