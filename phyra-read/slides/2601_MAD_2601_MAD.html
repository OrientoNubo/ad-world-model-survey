II

Abstract

Recent video diffusion models generate photorealistic, temporally coherent videos, yet they fall short as reliable world models for autonomous driving, where structured motion and physically consistent interactions are essential. Adapting these generalist video models to driving domains has shown promise but typically requires massive domain-specific data and costly fine-tuning. We propose an efficient adaptation framework that converts generalist video diffusion models into controllable driving world models with minimal supervision. The key idea is to decouple motion learning from appearance synthesis. First, the model is adapted to predict structured motion in a simplified form: videos of skeletonized agents and scene elements, focusing learning on physical and social plausibility. Then, the same backbone is reused to synthesize realistic RGB videos conditioned on these motion sequences, effectively "dressing" the motion with texture and lighting. This two-stage process mirrors a reasoning-rendering paradigm: first infer dynamics, then render appearance. Our experiments show this decoupled approach is exceptionally efficient: adapting SVD, we match prior SOTA models with less than 6% of their compute. Scaling to LTX, our MAD-LTX model outperforms all open-source competitors, and supports a comprehensive suite of text, ego, and object controls.

III

Research Overview

MAD proposes a two-stage framework: a Motion Forecaster (Fθ) generates abstract skeleton/pose videos of all dynamic agents; an Appearance Synthesizer (Sθ) renders final RGB video conditioned on those poses. Both stages fine-tune the same backbone using LoRA, reusing the model's native VAE for all conditioning signals. This design minimizes distributional shift between base model pretraining and driving fine-tuning.

Key Design Principles

  • Single backbone reuse: both Fθ and Sθ are LoRA adapters on the same pretrained VGM (SVD or LTX)
  • VAE-encoded conditioning: all conditioning signals (pose, first frame, ego-motion, object tracks) are encoded by the pretrained VAE; no new adapter networks
  • Native resolution training: operating at the base model's native FPS and resolution avoids out-of-distribution motion priors
  • Targeted noise injection: Gaussian noise σ ~ U(0, 0.3) applied only to skeleton latent regions in Sθ training to bridge train-inference domain gap

VI

Ego-Motion Visual Representation

A synthetic world (textured sphere + dust particles) is rendered from the ego-camera's perspective. Rotations appear as checkerboard motion; translations appear as parallax of dust particles. The resulting video is encoded by the pretrained VAE directly, requiring no separate trajectory encoder.

The apparent background motion is designed to correspond to static object motion in real driving video, enabling the VGM to correlate control input with expected scene dynamics.

Phyra's Judgment

Contributions vs. Claims

  • Supported: Efficiency gain (MAD-SVD vs. VISTA/GEM) — Fig. 6, compute table Sec. 4.2
  • Supported: Two-stage > direct fine-tuning — Table 1 planning + Fig. 5 human pref. (29 vs. 16 for 2B; 33 vs. 15 for 13B)
  • Supported: Pose repr. > HDMap / panoptic — Table 2
  • Unverified: Generalizability beyond OpenDV — no cross-dataset evaluation
  • Unverified: Generation of new agents entering scene — stated property, not evaluated

Fundamental Limitations

  • Final video quality is bounded by Fθ pose quality; targeted noise injection mitigates but does not eliminate the train-inference gap
  • Single-view (monocular) only; no multi-camera consistency modeling
  • Ego-motion visual rendering adds engineering overhead for integration with 6-DoF planning modules
  • Temporal errors in Fθ compound across 5-second horizon with no recurrent correction

XII

Open Questions & Improvement Directions

Outstanding Questions

  • Performance on datasets with distribution shift (adverse weather, night, non-US roads)?
  • Extension to multi-camera surround-view setups?
  • Quantitative relationship between Fθ pose quality and final RGB quality?
  • Can the 5-track object control limit be relaxed without degradation?

Improvement Directions

  • Closed-loop evaluation (high feasibility): connect MAD-LTX to a planner in CARLA or nuPlan via the ego-motion control interface
  • Iterative pose refinement (medium feasibility): lightweight diffusion-based refinement pass on blurry skeleton regions between Fθ and Sθ
  • Cross-dataset evaluation (high feasibility): same compute budget on nuScenes or Waymo to quantify domain transfer cost
