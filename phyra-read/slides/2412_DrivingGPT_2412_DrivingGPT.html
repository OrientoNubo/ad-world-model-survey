Unifying Driving World Modeling and Planning with Multi-modal Autoregressive Transformers

Yuntao Chen, Yuqi Wang, Zhaoxiang Zhang

NLPR / CASIA, School of AI / UCAS, HKISI / CAS

arXiv: 2412.18607 | 2024-12-24

Autonomous Driving World Model Autoregressive Transformer End-to-End Planning Next-Token Prediction

Core Idea

Treat autonomous driving as next-token prediction over interleaved image and action tokens.

A single autoregressive transformer simultaneously performs:

  • World Modeling: predicting future visual observations (image tokens)
  • Trajectory Planning: predicting future driving actions (action tokens)

This replaces the prevailing diffusion-based approach with a unified GPT-style sequence model.

Problem and Motivation

Pain Points of Existing Methods

Pain PointDescription
Diffusion models are modality-limitedVideo diffusion models operate on visual tokens; incorporating actions requires auxiliary mechanisms (cross-attention, channel modulation)
Fixed frame count degrades qualitySVD generates fixed frames; quality drops at longer horizons (FVD 418 at 16f to 1079 at 64f, Table 2)
Separate pipelinesWorld modeling and planning are typically trained as separate models, preventing end-to-end joint optimization

Key Argument

Autoregressive transformers naturally handle multi-modal sequential data via next-token prediction, making them inherently suitable for jointly modeling visual observations and driving actions in a single architecture.

Architecture Overview

[Front Camera Image o_t] | [VQ-VAE Encoder (LlamaGen)] --> Image Tokens z_t (576 or 2304 per frame) | [Driving Action a_t = (x, y, yaw)] | [Percentile Binning] --> Action Tokens q_t (3 per frame) | Interleaved Sequence: z_1, q_1, z_2, q_2, ..., z_T, q_T | [Autoregressive Transformer + Causal Attention + 1D RoPE] | Next-Token Prediction (Cross-Entropy Loss) | +-------------------+-------------------+ | | [VQ-VAE Decoder] [Action Unbinning] --> Generated Images --> 2D Transform --> Trajectory

Key Technical Components

Visual Tokenization

LlamaGen VQ-VAE with codebook size D = 16,384. Spatial downsampling rate S = 8 or 16 yields 2,304 or 576 tokens per 288 x 512 image.

Action Tokenization

Component-wise percentile binning: clamp each action component (x, y, yaw) at 1st/99th percentile, then uniformly bin into M = 128 bins. Separate vocabularies per component. Total action vocabulary: 3M = 384.

Unified Language

Total vocabulary: D + 3M = 16,768. Frame-wise 1D rotary position embedding applied to both modalities. Standard cross-entropy loss over the interleaved sequence.

Training Loss Decomposition

$$\mathcal{L} = \sum_i -\log p_\theta(x_i \mid x_{<i}) = \underbrace{-\log p_\theta(z_t \mid z_{<t}, q_{<t})}_{\text{World Modeling}} + \underbrace{-\log p_\theta(q_t \mid z_{\leq t}, q_{<t})}_{\text{Planning}}$$

Ablation Studies

Visual Tokenizer Choice (Table 4, p.7)

LlamaGen achieves the best FVD (68.40) and SSIM (0.652) among tested tokenizers (OpenMAGVIT2, Cosmos Tokenizer).

Prediction vs. Copy (Table 5, p.8)

Replacing predicted actions with copied history actions consistently degrades PDMS from 82.4 down to 53.5-73.1. This demonstrates the model learns to drive rather than memorize history trajectories.

Data Quality vs. Quantity (Table 6, p.8)

104k high-quality NAVSIM sequences (PDMS 82.4) outperform 651k nuPlan sequences (PDMS 74.6), indicating data quality matters more than quantity.

Position Embedding (Table 7, p.8)

1D rotary position embedding for both modalities (PDMS 82.4) far outperforms zero position embedding for actions (PDMS 65.3).

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • First to unify driving video generation and trajectory planning in a single autoregressive model
  • Autoregressive approach produces temporally consistent long videos (up to 32 seconds) where diffusion methods fail
  • Clean formulation: standard next-token prediction loss implicitly decomposes into world modeling and planning objectives

Weaknesses

  • Video generation baselines exclude driving-specific models (GAIA-1, Vista, DriveDreamer)
  • Planning baselines are weak; no comparison with published end-to-end planners on NAVSIM
  • No experiment demonstrates mutual benefit of joint training vs. separate models
  • No closed-loop evaluation; no inference latency analysis; no multi-camera support

Fundamental Limitations

  • Autoregressive generation is inherently slow; inference latency not analyzed
  • VQ-VAE discretization loses fine visual details critical for driving
  • Single front camera only; no natural extension to multi-view
  • No trajectory-level constraints (smoothness, kinematic feasibility)

Open Questions and Directions

Unanswered Questions

  • Does joint training actually improve either world modeling or planning compared to separate training?
  • What is the inference latency? Can it meet real-time requirements (10Hz+)?
  • How does DrivingGPT perform in closed-loop simulation?

Improvement Directions

  • Add joint-vs-separate training ablation (most critical missing experiment)
  • Extend to multi-camera input with view-specific position embeddings
  • Incorporate trajectory-level kinematic constraints into decoding
  • Apply speculative decoding to reduce autoregressive latency
  • Evaluate in closed-loop on nuPlan or CARLA
