ViDAR: Visual Point Cloud Forecasting enables Scalable Autonomous Driving

Zetong Yang, Li Chen, Yanan Sun, Hongyang Li

OpenDriveLab / Shanghai AI Lab | arXiv 2312.17655 | Dec 2023

Pre-training BEV Point Cloud Forecasting Autonomous Driving

Core Idea

ViDAR proposes visual point cloud forecasting as a pre-training task for autonomous driving. From historical multi-view camera images, the model predicts future LiDAR point clouds, learning semantics, 3D geometry, and temporal dynamics jointly.

The key insight: predicting future point clouds from past images forces the encoder to understand scene structure, object motion, and geometric projection in a single unified objective.

A novel Latent Rendering operator replaces differentiable ray-casting to solve the ray-shaped feature artifact problem, yielding discriminative geometric BEV features.

Architecture Overview

Multi-view Images (T=5, 6 cameras) | v [History Encoder] BEVFormer: RN101-DCN + FPN + 6 encoder layers | v F_bev (BEV features, 256 x 200 x 200) | v [Latent Rendering] 16-group, conditional prob. + feature expectation | v F_hat_bev (Geometric BEV features) | v [Future Decoder] 6-layer Transformer, auto-regressive, ego-motion conditioned | v F_t (Future BEV, t = 1..6 at 0.5s) | v [Projection + Ray-casting] -> Point clouds (supervised by GT LiDAR)

Three components: (1) History Encoder extracts BEV embeddings, (2) Latent Rendering converts to geometric features, (3) Future Decoder predicts future BEV features auto-regressively.

Latent Rendering Operator

The core technical contribution. Replaces differentiable ray-casting with a learned rendering process in latent space.

Why not direct ray-casting?

BEV grids along the same ray map to the same pixel in the image, producing identical feature responses (ray-shaped features, Fig.3-a). This reduces discriminability and hurts downstream performance by 3.91 NDS (Table 1, p.4).

Latent Rendering solution

Step 1: Estimate independent probability map $\mathbf{p}$ from $\mathcal{F}_{\text{bev}}$.

Step 2: Compute conditional probability $\hat{\mathbf{p}}^{(i)}$ that grid $i$ is the first occupied cell along its ray.

Step 3: Feature expectation aggregates ray-wise features weighted by $\hat{\mathbf{p}}$.

Step 4: Multiply to get geometric features $\hat{\mathcal{F}}_{\text{bev}} = \hat{\mathbf{p}} \cdot \hat{\mathcal{F}}$.

$$\hat{\mathbf{p}}^{(i)} = \left[\prod_j (1 - \mathbf{p}^{(\mathbf{x}^{(i,j)})})\right] \mathbf{p}^{(\mathbf{g}^{(i)})}$$

Multi-group Latent Rendering (G=16) runs in parallel on different channel subsets, increasing feature diversity (Fig.4, p.4).

Future Decoder

A Transformer-based module that auto-regressively predicts future BEV features conditioned on ego-motion.

  • Input: last BEV features $\hat{\mathcal{F}}_{t-1}$ and ego-motion condition $\mathbf{e}_t$
  • Ego-motion encoded via MLP, added to future BEV queries
  • 6 transformer layers: Deformable Self-Attention + Temporal Cross-Attention + FFN (Fig.5, p.5)
  • Reference coordinates recomputed per step to handle ego-vehicle motion
  • Predicts 6 future timesteps (3 seconds at 0.5s intervals)

Loss: ray-wise cross-entropy on occupancy volume, maximizing response at ground-truth point positions (Eq.7, p.5).

Annotation Efficiency

ViDAR reduces the dependence on 3D annotations (Fig.6, p.8):

  • With only 50% of labeled data, ViDAR-pretrained BEVFormer achieves 39.4% mAP, surpassing full supervision (37.7% mAP) by 1.7% (Fig.6, p.8)
  • At 1/8 subset, ViDAR improves mAP by 7.3% over ImageNet-cls baseline
  • The improvement increases as available annotations decrease, confirming ViDAR's value for low-annotation regimes

Limitations and Open Questions

Structural Limitations

  • Requires paired Image-LiDAR data for pre-training; cannot use pure camera-only datasets
  • Auto-regressive Future Decoder accumulates prediction errors over time; no analysis beyond 3 seconds
  • Scalability claim is not validated beyond nuScenes (1,000 sequences)

Open Questions

  • Can LiDAR supervision be replaced by self-supervised depth (stereo, SfM)?
  • How does computational overhead of 16-group Latent Rendering compare to contrastive pre-training?
  • Does performance scale with more unlabeled Image-LiDAR data?

Key Takeaways

  1. Visual point cloud forecasting is a well-designed pre-training proxy that jointly captures semantics, 3D geometry, and temporal dynamics from camera-only input.
  2. The Latent Rendering operator resolves the ray-shaped feature artifact inherent in differentiable ray-casting, improving NDS by 7.38% over the naive approach (Table 1, p.4).
  3. ViDAR delivers consistent gains across 7 downstream tasks spanning perception, prediction, and planning, with the largest improvements in tracking (+7.6 AMOTA) and detection (+4.39 mAP) on UniAD (Table 10, p.8).
