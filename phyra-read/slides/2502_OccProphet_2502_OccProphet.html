OccProphet

Pushing Efficiency Frontier of Camera-Only 4D Occupancy Forecasting with Observer-Forecaster-Refiner Framework

Junliang Chen*, Huaiyuan Xu*, Yi Wang, Lap-Pui Chau

The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

ICLR 2025  |  arXiv: 2502.15180

Core Contribution

OccProphet is a camera-only 4D occupancy forecasting framework that achieves a Pareto improvement over the state-of-the-art Cam4DOcc: higher accuracy with dramatically lower cost.

Efficiency Gains (vs. Cam4DOcc)

Parameters: 82% reduction (370M to 82M)

FLOPs: 69% reduction (6434G to 1985G)

Memory: 58% reduction (57GB to 24GB)

Speed: 2.6x faster (1.7 to 4.5 FPS)

Accuracy Gains

IoU_f improvement: 4% to 18% across three datasets and three evaluation tasks (Tables 2-4, p.7-8)

Consistent on nuScenes, Lyft-Level5, nuScenes-Occupancy

Source: Table 6 (p.9) for efficiency; Tables 2-4 for accuracy

Problem and Motivation

Task: Camera-only 4D occupancy forecasting predicts future 3D occupied states from surround-view video, enabling autonomous vehicles to anticipate environmental changes for safe planning.

Limitations of Existing Methods

Method TypeLimitation
BEV-based (FIERY, BEVerse)Loses 3D geometry by compressing height; restricted to 2D plane forecasting
LiDAR-based (UnO, Khurana et al.)Relies on expensive LiDAR point clouds, increasing hardware cost
Cam4DOcc (SOTA camera-only)High computational cost (370M params, 57GB memory, 6434 GFLOPs) makes on-vehicle deployment infeasible

Key insight: Most 3D space is unoccupied. Performing spatio-temporal interactions on downsampled compact features can preserve essential information while dramatically reducing computation.

Architecture: Observer-Forecaster-Refiner

Multi-frame surround-view images (6 cameras x T frames) | [Shared ResNet Encoder] -> 2D features | [2D-to-3D Lifting + Voxel Pooling] -> 3D voxel features | [Concat T frames + 6-DoF ego poses] -> F_motion | [Observer: E4A + TAF] -> O_obs (spatio-temporal repr.) | [Forecaster: Condition Gen. + Conditional Forecaster] -> F_future | [Refiner: E4A reuse] -> Refined future features | [Predictor: Occ Head + Flow Head] -> Occupancy + Flow

Source: Figure 3, p.4

Observer: E4A + Tripling-Attention Fusion

Efficient 4D Aggregation (E4A)

Downsample-interact-upsample with residual:

$$Q' = Q + \mathcal{F}(Q)$$

Outperforms BEV and TPV representations with fewer parameters (67M vs. 102M/183M, Table 1 p.5).

Tripling-Attention Fusion (TAF)

Decomposes 3D features into three branches:

Scene: GAP_3D, global context (1x1x1)

Height: GAP_2D + Conv1D, vertical details (1x1xZ)

BEV: GAP_1D + W-MSA, spatial layout (XxYx1)

$$U_\text{HF} = \text{TA}_\text{scene}(S) \oplus \text{TA}_\text{height}(H) \oplus \text{TA}_\text{BEV}(B)$$

Source: Equations 1-4 (p.5), Figure 5 (p.5)

Forecaster and Refiner

Conditional Forecaster

Generates adaptive weights conditioned on the overall environmental context:

$$F'_\text{cond} = \text{Act}(\text{Norm}(\text{Linear}(\text{GAP}_{3\text{D}}(O_\text{obs}))))$$

$$W_\text{cond} = \text{Linear}(F_\text{cond})$$

Adapts to varying traffic complexity (e.g., crowded intersection vs. empty highway) by generating scenario-specific prediction weights.

Refiner

Reuses the E4A architecture to refine forecasted features by incorporating cross-frame interactions and historical observations:

$$V = \text{E4A}([O_\text{obs}, F_\text{future}])$$

Contribution: +0.8% IoU_f (Table 5, p.9). Marginal but consistent.

Source: Equations 5-7 (p.6), Figure 6 (p.6)

Strengths

  • Genuine Pareto improvement: better accuracy at lower cost across all metrics
  • E4A is a reusable, efficient module (Observer + Refiner share it)
  • Consistent gains across 3 datasets prevent dataset-specific tuning concerns

Weaknesses

  • Refiner contributes only +0.8% IoU, questionable architectural overhead
  • Cross-domain generalization shows degradation (Lyft to nuScenes, Section A.5.1)
  • Linear Forecaster limits nonlinear dynamics modeling; fixed 2s horizon

Limitations and Open Directions

Structural Limitations

  • Linear projection in Forecaster cannot capture nonlinear dynamics
  • Fixed forecasting horizon (4 future frames at 2s)
  • No semantic reasoning about agent intent
  • E4A downsampling inherently loses small-object details

Potential Improvements

  • Replace linear Forecaster with lightweight autoregressive model
  • Multi-scale E4A for better small-object preservation
  • Incorporate motion priors (optical flow)
  • Action-conditioned forecasting for planning integration
