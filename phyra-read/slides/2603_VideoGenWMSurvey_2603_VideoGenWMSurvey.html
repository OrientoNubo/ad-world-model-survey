Video Generation Models as World Models:
Efficient Paradigms, Architectures and Algorithms
Muyang He · Hanzhong Guo · Junxiong Lin · Yizhou Yu  |  University of Hong Kong  |  arXiv 2603.28489 · 2026-03-30
Survey Video Generation World Models Diffusion Efficient Inference Autonomous Driving Embodied AI 22 pages · 248 refs

Abstract

The rapid evolution of video generation has enabled models to simulate complex physical dynamics and long-horizon causalities, positioning them as potential world simulators. However, a critical gap still remains between the theoretical capacity for world simulation and the heavy computational costs of spatiotemporal modeling. To address this, we comprehensively and systematically review video generation frameworks and techniques that consider efficiency as a crucial requirement for practical world modeling. We introduce a novel taxonomy in three dimensions: efficient modeling paradigms, efficient network architectures, and efficient inference algorithms. We further show that bridging this efficiency gap directly empowers interactive applications such as autonomous driving, embodied AI, and game simulation. Finally, we identify emerging research frontiers in efficient video-based world modeling, arguing that efficiency is a fundamental prerequisite for evolving video generators into general-purpose, real-time, and robust world simulators.

Training objective
$$L_\text{simple} = \mathbb{E}_{x_0,t,\epsilon}\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2$$
Flow Matching (conditional)
$$L_\text{CFM} = \mathbb{E}\|v_\theta(x_t,t) - (x_1-z_0)\|^2$$
Auto-Regressive
$$p(x) = \prod_{i=1}^N p(x_i|x_{<i})$$

Efficient Modeling: Key Methods

Step-Reduction Distillation

Train student to match teacher in one macro-step. GPD [53] reduces Wan from 48 to 6 steps.

Consistency Distillation

Maps any trajectory point to origin $x_0$. VideoLCM, AnimateLCM enable real-time synthesis.

Adversarial Distillation

Minimize $D(p_S \| p_T)$ at distribution level. Seaweed-APT achieves one-step real-time generation.

AR World Modeling

Genie: spatiotemporal tokenizer + AR dynamics + latent action model. iVideoGPT: action/reward/obs unified sequence.

Hybrid AR-Diffusion

AR temporal rollout + diffusion per-chunk. FramePack compresses historical context by importance.

Streaming Causal Diffusion

Diffusion Forcing [69] → Self Forcing [70] → Rolling Forcing [71]: progressive addressing of train-test mismatch and long-horizon drift.

Efficient Architecture: Key Directions

VAE & Hierarchical

  • DC-VideoGen: 64× spatial, 4× temporal compression
  • REGEN: generative decoder for 32× temporal compression
  • Waver / FlashVideo: cascade to 1080p from low-res DiT
  • SRDiffusion: large model at high-noise, small model at low-noise

Memory Mechanisms

  • Visual: FramePack, WorldPack (hierarchical frame compression)
  • Spatial: EvoWorld (spherical 3D), VMem (surfel-indexed map)
  • Compressed: SVI (Error Replay), LoViC (FlexFormer)
  • Implicit: TTT layers in DiT, LaCT (50k+ token context)

Efficient Attention

  • Sparse: SVG [105], SVG2 [106], VMoBA [110] — structural sparsity
  • Windowed: LongLive (frame sink + short window), STA (hardware-aligned sliding tile)
  • Linear: SANA-Video (ReLU linear), SLA (sparse+linear hybrid)
  • SSM: LinGen (Mamba2 + Temporal Swin, linear scaling)

RoPE Extrapolation

  • RIFLEx: frequency shifting → 3× length extrapolation (training-free)
  • UltraViCo: decay factor to suppress attention dispersion
  • Infinity-RoPE: Block-Relativistic RoPE for infinite simulation

Highlights

  • Three-dimensional taxonomy (modeling / architecture / inference) provides a structured decomposition absent in prior surveys.
  • Table III maps 80+ papers to a (domain × task type) matrix, directly usable as a literature index.
  • DDPM, flow matching, AR formulations unified in common notation for cross-paradigm comparison.
  • Table I provides the only unified hardware-controlled caching benchmark in the survey.

Weaknesses

No unified re-evaluation. Tables I and II aggregate numbers from different papers with different hardware and protocols; direct cross-row comparison is unsupported.
Section VII (talking head, content creation, 4D scenes) is weakly connected to the efficiency-for-world-modeling thesis, reducing structural coherence.
The "first systematic review" claim (p.2) is stated without comparing against existing surveys to demonstrate the gap.
Distillation section does not report wall-clock latency or memory cost for most cited methods; efficiency claim is not quantified.

Citations Worth Tracking

[69] Diffusion Forcing

Foundational training paradigm for causal streaming diffusion with independent noise levels per token.

[70,71] Self/Rolling Forcing

Bridges train-test mismatch for long-horizon streaming; enables real-time multi-minute generation.

[145] PreciseCache

Step-wise + block-wise caching; provides the only unified hardware benchmark (Table I) in the survey.

[147] WorldCache

Caching for world models specifically; addresses heterogeneous token behavior and non-uniform temporal dynamics.

[50] iVideoGPT

Scalable AR world model unifying observations, actions, rewards; clean baseline for the AR paradigm.

[219] Fast-WAM

Provides controlled evidence that video co-training objective (not future imagination at test time) is the source of world model gains.

Open Questions

Can distillation-based efficiency (few-step generation) compose with streaming causal approaches to yield a model that is both fast-per-step and stable over minute-scale rollouts?
What architectural principle governs the stability-plasticity trade-off in spatial memory mechanisms (IV.E): when should a global map be updated vs. retained?
What is the principled boundary of "video-based world model" that includes talking head (VII.A) but presumably excludes generic video editing? The survey does not state this explicitly.
