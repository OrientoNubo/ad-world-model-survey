Think2Drive

Efficient Reinforcement Learning by Thinking with Latent World Model for Autonomous Driving (in CARLA-v2)

Qifeng Li*, Xiaosong Jia*, Shaobo Wang, Junchi Yan
Shanghai Jiao Tong University | arXiv: 2402.16720v2 | July 2024
Model-based RL World Model Autonomous Driving CARLA v2 Neural Planner

Problem and Motivation

CARLA Leaderboard v2 introduces 39 quasi-realistic corner-case scenarios absent in v1, with 7-10 km routes containing ~60 scenarios each. As of this paper's writing, no method had achieved success on CARLA v2.

Rule-based planners fail because their decision trees cannot cover all corner cases. Imitation learning methods lack sufficient demonstrations. Model-free RL (e.g., Roach [45]) suffers from data inefficiency given CARLA v2's complexity.

The core insight: model-based RL with a latent world model can learn environment transitions efficiently, enabling the planner to "think" in latent space instead of interacting with the expensive physical simulator.

Method Overview

Think2Drive uses DreamerV3's RSSM architecture as the world model backbone, adapted for autonomous driving with BEV semantic segmentation input.

$$s_t \leftarrow F_\theta^{Enc}(x_t), \quad a_t \leftarrow \pi(s_t), \quad s_{t+1} \leftarrow F_\theta^{Pre}(s_t, a_t)$$

The planner rolls out in the latent state space of the world model, generating actions without interacting with the physical simulator. This enables hundreds of parallel rollouts at a fraction of the computational cost.

ComponentDescription
InputBEV masks (128x128x34), 4 historical timesteps, speed, action history
World ModelRSSM (DreamerV3): sequence model + encoder + dynamics/reward/termination predictors + decoder
PlannerActor-critic architecture, 30 discretized actions
Parameters104M total (GRU: 512 units, CNN multiplier: 96, Dense: 512, MLP: 5 layers)

Seven Engineering Bricks

The paper identifies 7 AD-specific challenges for model-based RL and proposes corresponding solutions:

Brick 1: Policy Reset

Randomly re-initialize planner weights mid-training to escape local optima. The world model provides dense rewards, mitigating cold-start. (p. 7)

Brick 2: Auto Scenario Generation

Split routes into short segments with dense scenarios; build CornerCaseRepo benchmark. (p. 7)

Brick 3: Termination-Priority Replay

Sample termination-adjacent frames with 50% probability to focus on valuable transitions. (p. 7)

Brick 4: Steering Cost

Penalize steering changes for heading stabilization. (p. 8)

Brick 5: Curriculum Warm-up

Pre-train on simple lane-following before tackling complex scenarios. (p. 8)

Brick 6: Incremental Train Ratio

Planner training ratio reaches 4x world model rate at convergence. (p. 8)

Brick 7: Async CARLA Wrapper

Asynchronous reloading + parallel execution to reduce reset time. (p. 8)

CornerCaseRepo Benchmark and Weighted Driving Score

CornerCaseRepo

1,600 training + 390 evaluation routes. Each route contains one scenario type with length < 300m. Enables per-scenario evaluation. 40 routes per scenario in training, 10 in evaluation. (p. 10)

Weighted Driving Score (WDS)

$$\text{WDS} = \text{RC} \times \prod_i \text{penalty}_i^{n_i}$$

Where $n_i = \frac{\text{Num. Infractions}}{\text{Scenario Density}}$. This corrects for the counter-intuitive property where longer routes yield lower scores at the same infraction rate. (p. 10, Eq. 7)

Performance Across 39 Scenarios (Table 2, p. 9)

Think2Drive achieves success rates between 0.61 and 1.0 across all 39 CARLA v2 scenarios. The hardest scenarios are AccidentTwoWays (0.61), EnterActorFlows (0.65), and NonSignalizedRightTurn (0.65).

The easiest scenarios (success rate = 1.0) include HardBrake and HighwayCutIn. Most junction-negotiation scenarios achieve 0.75-0.95 success rates.

RouteObstacles-type scenarios (Accident, Construction, HazardAtSideLane and their TwoWays variants) are the most challenging category, requiring sophisticated lane negotiation within tight time windows on two-way roads.

Critical Assessment

Strengths

  • First successful model-based RL for CARLA v2, a genuinely hard benchmark
  • Practical training budget: 3 days on a single A6000 GPU
  • CornerCaseRepo and WDS are useful contributions to the community
  • Thorough ablation validates each engineering decision

Limitations

  • Relies on privileged information (ground-truth BEV), not raw sensors
  • World model architecture is DreamerV3 without structural novelty
  • 7 "bricks" are engineering solutions; generalization is unclear
  • 30-action discretization is coarse; no sensitivity analysis
  • PDM-lite [21] later achieved similar results with rule-based planning
