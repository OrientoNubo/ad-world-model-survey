Motivation

Autonomous driving requires predicting future events and anticipating sensory changes. World models, inspired by human cognitive processes, learn internal representations of the environment to predict future states and support decision-making.

Key Problem

Despite rapid growth in world model research (publications rose from under 5 in 2015 to over 50 in 2024, per Fig. 1, p. 2), no comprehensive survey had systematically organized foundational principles, methodological advances, and practical applications of world models specifically for autonomous driving.

Pain Points Addressed

  • Data scarcity: collecting and annotating driving data is expensive and legally constrained (p. 8)
  • Sim-to-real gap: simulation environments diverge from real-world physics, sensor noise, and rare events (pp. 10-11)
  • Computational scalability: long-term memory and high-dimensional sensory processing remain bottlenecks (p. 11)

Policy Output
  • Perception: compresses environmental inputs (images, video, LiDAR, text) into manageable latent representations
  • Memory: replays past experiences; synthesizes short-term and long-term context for adaptation
  • World Model: estimates missing information about current state and predicts future states
  • Control/Action: evaluates predictions to determine optimal action sequences

Core Architecture: RSSM

The Recurrent State Space Model (Hafner et al., PlaNet, 2019) decomposes latent states into deterministic and stochastic components for forward prediction. It serves as the backbone of the Dreamer model family.

Key Formulation (pp. 4-5)

Deterministic path: $h_t = \text{GRU}(h_{t-1}, \text{MLP}(\text{concat}[z_{t-1}, a_t]))$
Prior: $p(z_t \mid z_{<t}, a_{\leq t}) = \text{MLP}(h_t)$
Training: maximize ELBO (Eq. 5, p. 5)

The deterministic component (via GRU) preserves information continuity; the stochastic component captures uncertainty. This hybrid structure balances precision and flexibility (p. 4).

Core Architecture: JEPA

The Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (LeCun) shifts prediction from pixel space to representation space, achieving higher efficiency by filtering noise and focusing on essential predictive content.

Key Formulation (pp. 5-6)

Energy function: $E_w(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}; \mathbf{z}; \theta) = \|\mathbf{s}_y - \text{Pred}(\mathbf{s}_x, \mathbf{z} : \phi)\|_2^2 + \lambda\|\mathbf{z}\|_2^2$
Optimization: Lagrangian with second-order derivatives (Eq. 8, p. 5)

Dual encoders abstract input (x) and target (y) into representations; a latent variable (z) manages uncertainty. The regularization term prevents complexity overfitting.

Application Domains (Fig. 5, p. 8)

1. Driving Scenario Generation

World models generate diverse driving scenarios from historical data, addressing data scarcity. Key models: GAIA-1 (counterfactual reasoning, generates unseen driving concepts), DriveDreamer (nuScenes-based, includes HD maps and 3D boxes), WorldDreamer (spatiotemporal patchwise attention).

2. Planning and Control

Models learn driving dynamics to evaluate future trajectories and refine control. MILE improved CARLA Driving Score from 46 to 61 (expert baseline: 88, p. 9) via model-based imitation learning. TrafficBots uses CVAE to predict individual agent actions.

3. End-to-End Driving

Integration of perception, prediction, and control into unified frameworks. ADriver-1 combines MLLM + VDM for continuous prediction cycles. OccWorld and Think2Drive use 3D occupancy prediction for planning.

Open Challenges

  • Sim-to-Real Gap: discrepancies in physics, sensor noise, and rare event frequency between simulation and real-world deployment (pp. 10-11)
  • Computational Scalability: long-term memory integration and real-time inference for high-dimensional sensory data remain bottlenecks (p. 11)
  • Ethical Considerations: decision-making accountability, privacy (GDPR/CCPA compliance), and urban ecosystem impact require multi-stakeholder frameworks (pp. 11-12)

Future Directions Identified by Authors

  • Cognitive co-piloting: evolving world models toward human-like cognitive frameworks (p. 12)
  • Integration of external memory models and iterative learning strategies
  • Harmonizing vehicles with urban ecosystems for adaptive, responsive behaviors (p. 12)

Phyra's Judgment

Strengths

  • Table I (p. 7) is the most comprehensive catalog of world model variants (2018-2024) with core structures and key ideas
  • Mathematical formulations for RSSM and JEPA (pp. 4-6) make foundational concepts accessible
  • Chronological overview (Fig. 6) with input modality and task mapping provides clear field orientation

Weaknesses

  • No quantitative comparison across surveyed models; relative performance claims are unverifiable
  • 40+ models in 17 pages: most receive only 2-3 sentences, sacrificing analytical depth
  • Taxonomy is primarily chronological/structural without systematic trade-off analysis or failure mode categorization
