Cosmos-Drive-Dreams

Scalable Synthetic Driving Data Generation with World Foundation Models

Xuanchi Ren*, Yifan Lu*, Tianshi Cao*, Ruiyuan Gao* et al. | NVIDIA | arXiv 2506.09042 | 2025-06-18
World Foundation Model Synthetic Data Autonomous Driving Video Diffusion Multi-view LiDAR

Core Problem

Autonomous driving systems require large-scale, multi-view, temporally consistent training data with fine-grained annotations. Two critical bottlenecks impede progress:

Data Scarcity

Safety-critical long-tail events (extreme weather, sudden pedestrian crossings, unusual road layouts) are inherently rare in real-world data collection. Models trained on naturally occurring data fail to generalize to these corner cases (p.2).

Existing Solutions Fall Short

Physics-based simulators lack photorealism and require extensive engineering. Reconstruction-based methods cannot modify lighting or weather. Prior generative approaches struggle with rare events due to training distribution bias (p.20-21).

Solution: Cosmos-Drive-Dreams Pipeline

A four-step synthetic data generation pipeline built on Cosmos-Drive, a suite of models post-trained from NVIDIA Cosmos-1 World Foundation Model (p.2-3, Fig. 2).

1
Render pixel-aligned HDMap condition videos from structured labels (3D cuboids, lane lines) or annotate in-the-wild videos via Cosmos-7B-Annotate-Sample-AV
2
Rewrite text prompts for weather/time diversity (Qwen2.5-7B) and generate single-view videos via Cosmos-Transfer1-7B-Sample-AV with ControlNet conditioning on HDMap/LiDAR
3
Expand single-view to 6-camera multi-view via Cosmos-7B-Single2Multiview-Sample-AV (MV Transformer Blocks with joint self-attention across views)
4
VLM-based rejection sampling (Qwen2.5-VL-7B) filters artifacts; ~3% of samples discarded to ensure dataset quality

Key Technical Contributions

Multi-View Architecture

Clean input-view tokens and noisy multi-view tokens are concatenated with view embeddings and binary I/O indicators along the channel dimension, then joined along the sequence dimension. Self-attention operates jointly across all views; cross-attention with text is per-view (p.6, Fig. 7).

LiDAR Sensor Modeling

LiDAR points are projected to spherical coordinates with sensor-specific correction:

$$r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}, \; \phi = \arctan2(y,x), \; \theta = \arcsin(z/r)$$

Elevation angle profiles and azimuth zig-zag patterns eliminate ghost artifacts. Range maps use float32 tokenization and 4x row repetition for sparsity (p.9-10).

Annotation Inversion

Cosmos-7B-Annotate-Sample-AV replaces text embeddings with trainable context embeddings in cross-attention layers, enabling modality-specific conditioning (HDMap, LiDAR) without text prompts. This allows internet-sourced driving videos to be auto-annotated for SDG input (p.7, Fig. 9).

Scaling Behavior

Data Volume Scaling

SDG benefits are most pronounced in the low-data regime. With only 2k real clips, adding SDG yields +6.0% F1 on the general test set (Fig. 15, p.15). The gap narrows with more real data but persists up to 20k clips.

SDG Ratio Scaling

Performance improves monotonically as SDG ratio increases from 0 to 3x for policy learning. For 3D detection, SDG volume also correlates positively with performance (Fig. 16-Right, p.17).

Targeted SDG

Adding VRU/left-turn targeted SDG (less than 2% of training data) improves corner-case minADE on RDS-Bench[VRU/left] without degrading general driving performance (Fig. 20-Right, p.19). This validates scenario-specific data augmentation as a practical strategy.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Complete SDG pipeline with consistent downstream gains across 3 AV tasks (p.14-19)
  • Annotation inversion model enables unlabeled internet video to become SDG input (p.6-7)
  • LiDAR generation with proper sensor modeling produces artifact-free range maps (+4.2% mAP, p.18)

Weaknesses

  • No comparison with generative SDG baselines (MagicDrive, GAIA-2, Drive-WM); only Albumentations tested (p.16)
  • Core training data (RDS, 3.6M clips) is proprietary NVIDIA data (p.4)
  • VLM rejection sampling error rates unreported (p.14)

Open Questions

  • How does Cosmos-Drive-Dreams compare to MagicDrive, Drive-WM, or GAIA-2 on identical downstream benchmarks?
  • What is the per-clip generation cost (GPU-hours) for the full pipeline, and how does this scale?
  • What are the false-positive/false-negative rates of the VLM rejection sampling module?
  • How does generation quality and downstream impact change at production-grade resolutions (1920x1080+)?

Improvement Directions

  • Head-to-head benchmarking against generative SDG methods with identical training protocols
  • Uncertainty-driven adaptive SDG allocation instead of uniform weather rewriting
  • FID/FVD quality metrics and feature-space domain gap measurement for continuous quality monitoring
