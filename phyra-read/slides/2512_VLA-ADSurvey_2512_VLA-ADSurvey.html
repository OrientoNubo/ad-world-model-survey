Research Overview

This survey characterizes VLA models for autonomous driving, tracing the evolution from early Vision-Action (VA) approaches to modern Vision-Language-Action (VLA) frameworks. It proposes a hierarchical taxonomy distinguishing End-to-End VLA (single-model perception-reasoning-planning) from Dual-System VLA (slow VLM + fast executor), with subclasses covering textual vs. numerical action generation and explicit vs. implicit guidance. Datasets, benchmarks, challenges, and future directions are reviewed. 135+ methods cataloged across Tables 1 and 3.

VLA Problem Formulation (Sec 2)

General Formulation

The VLA framework is formalized as:

$$a_t = H(F(x \mid \theta))$$

where $x$ is multimodal input at timestamp $t$, $F(\cdot)$ is the VLM backbone parameterized by $\theta$, and $H(\cdot)$ is the action generation head.

Action Space Representations

Discrete Waypoints (Eq. 2)

$$a_t = \{(x_i, y_i)\}_{i=1}^{\Phi}$$

Continuous Trajectory (Eq. 3)

$$a_t = (v(t),\, \kappa(t)),\quad t \in [0,T]$$

Control Signals (Eq. 4)

$$a_t = (\delta_t,\, \tau_t,\, \beta_t)$$

Language Tokens (Eq. 5)

$$a_t = \{w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_T\},\quad w_i \in V$$

Challenges & Future Directions (Sec 6)

Current Challenges

  • Real-time latency: VLM inference > 50ms; sub-50ms requirement unmet for safety-critical deployment
  • No domain-specific foundation model: general VLMs lack driving-specific physics and multi-sensor fusion priors
  • Long-tail generalization: VLM perception priors do not automatically transfer to action space robustness
  • Data cost: vision-action-language triplets at scale are expensive; sim-to-real gap remains
  • Hallucination: language rationales are generated artifacts, not faithful causal explanations; spurious justifications
  • Long-horizon coherence: short context windows cause inconsistent decisions in multi-agent dynamic scenes

Future Directions

  • Unified VLA + World Model: integrate predictive world models (Sec 3.2) into VLA for proactive planning
  • Richer multimodal fusion: early fusion of LiDAR, Radar, event cameras, HD maps with language grounding
  • Socially aware reasoning: commonsense intent, conventions, and causal relationships beyond annotations
  • Continual onboard learning: incremental adaptation without catastrophic forgetting
  • Human-AI interaction: instruction fidelity, preference alignment, transparent rationale generation
  • Standardized evaluation: unified protocols covering long-tail, safety, language alignment jointly

Open Questions

  • What is the minimum VLM parameter count that preserves driving-relevant reasoning while meeting sub-50ms latency for closed-loop deployment?
  • Do chain-of-thought rationales produced by textual VLA models causally influence trajectory quality, or are they post-hoc?
  • Can implicit representation transfer (Sec 4.2.2) fully close the gap with online VLM inference, or does real-time language grounding provide non-distillable benefits?
  • Is the PDMS/RFS divergence from L2 metrics attributable to benchmark distributional differences, or genuine behavioral differences between methods?
