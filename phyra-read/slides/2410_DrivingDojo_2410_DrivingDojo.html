Problem and Motivation

Driving world models require diverse, interaction-rich video data to fulfill their potential as real-world simulators. Existing driving datasets were designed for perception or prediction and fall short in three dimensions:

GapDescriptionSource
Incomplete actionsnuScenes and ONCE are dominated by lane-keeping; turns, lane changes, and emergency braking are rareFig. 3a, p.4
No multi-agent interactionsDatasets curated for perception lack cut-in/cut-off, yielding, and overtaking scenariosp.2

DrivingDojo fills these gaps by curating 18k videos from fleet data, organized into three targeted subsets.

Data Curation Pipeline

Six data sources feed into the curation process (p.6):

  1. Intervention data from safety inspectors during vehicle operation
  2. Emergency brake data from automatic emergency braking
  3. Randomly sampled 30-second general driving clips
  4. Selected distinct scenarios: traffic lights, crossings, barriers, lane changes, pedestrian interactions
  5. Manually sorted rare data: foreign objects on road, falling/rolling items
  6. GPT-4o annotation for open-world knowledge descriptions

PII removal is performed using license plate and face detectors with manual double-checking (p.6). Dataset released under CC BY-NC 4.0 license (p.16).

Action Instruction Following (AIF) Task

Given an initial image $I_t$ and an action sequence $\{A_t, ..., A_{t+k}\}$, the world model predicts future frames:

$$\{I_t, I_{t+1}, ..., I_{t+k}\} = f_\theta(I_t, \{A_t, A_{t+1}, ..., A_{t+k}\})$$

Each action $A_t = (\Delta x_t, \Delta y_t)$ represents the next frame's camera position in the current camera coordinate system (Eq. 1, p.7).

The AIF error metric uses SfM (COLMAP) to estimate ego trajectories from generated videos and compares them against input actions:

$$(E_x^{AIF}, E_y^{AIF}) = \frac{\sum_{i=0}^{k} |A_{t+i} - \tilde{A}_{t+i} \cdot \hat{S}|}{k+1}$$

$\hat{S}$ is a scale factor estimated from the first $N=10$ frames to align SfM trajectories with metric scale (Eq. 2, p.7).

Baseline Model Architecture

The baseline extends Stable Video Diffusion (SVD-XT) for action-conditioned generation:

  • Action sequence encoded via MLP into a 1024-d vector
  • Concatenated with first-frame image feature and fed into U-Net
  • Training: EDM framework, 576x320 resolution, 30 frames, AdamW (lr=1e-5)
  • Hardware: 16x A100 (80G), batch size 32, 50K iterations
  • Inference: DDIM sampler, 25 steps

Classifier-free guidance dropout: 20% action feature dropout (p.22).

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • First dataset to simultaneously cover action completeness, multi-agent interplay, and open-world knowledge for world model training (Table 1, p.3)
  • Systematic curation from a 7,500-hour fleet data pool using multiple complementary strategies (p.6)
  • AIF benchmark provides a quantitative way to evaluate action controllability beyond visual quality metrics

Weaknesses

  • Only SVD tested as baseline; no comparison with GAIA-1, Drive-WM, Vista, or other driving world models
  • AIF metric accuracy depends on COLMAP SfM, which is not validated against ground truth on generated videos
  • No ablation on the contribution of each dataset subset (Action/Interplay/Open)
  • Single-camera, front-view only; limits applicability for multi-view world model research (p.10)

Fundamental Limitations

  • Single-camera constraint: Front-view only data cannot support multi-view or surround-view world model research, which is increasingly the norm for autonomous driving
  • AIF metric circularity: SfM accuracy on generated videos degrades as generation quality decreases, making the metric less reliable for weaker models
  • Fleet distribution bias: All data comes from Meituan delivery vehicles in Chinese cities; road types, traffic patterns, and driving styles may not generalize globally
  • Short-horizon generation: Baseline produces 30 frames (~6s at 5fps), insufficient for planning-relevant horizons

Open Questions and Future Directions

  • How much does each subset (Action/Interplay/Open) individually contribute to performance?
  • What is the SfM trajectory estimation error on real DrivingDojo videos, establishing a baseline for interpreting AIF values?
  • Does low AIF error translate to better closed-loop driving performance?
  • How do other world model architectures (autoregressive, 3D-aware) perform when trained on DrivingDojo?

Improvement Directions

  1. Multi-model benchmarking: Train GAIA-1, Vista, and autoregressive models on DrivingDojo (high feasibility)
  2. Subset ablation: Isolate the value of each subset to guide future curation efforts (high feasibility)
  3. Multi-camera extension: Leverage side/rear cameras from fleet vehicles (medium feasibility)
  4. AIF metric validation: Compare SfM estimates against GPS/IMU ground truth (medium feasibility)
